5th lunar month, 5th day · A festival五月初五 · 一个节

Duanwu · Dragon Boat端午 · 龙舟

Zongzi: glutinous rice tied in bamboo leaf. The Yangtze decides whether yours is salty or sweet, and which side you're on says everything about where you grew up.粽子：糯米裹竹叶。长江决定你家的是咸的还是甜的，你站哪一边，说出你在哪里长大。

The Dragon Boat Festival (端午节, Duan Wu Jie) falls on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month — late May or early June in the Gregorian calendar. It commemorates the suicide of Qu Yuan, a Warring-States-era poet and patriot who drowned himself in the Miluo River in 278 BCE after his counsel was rejected by the state of Chu. The legend goes that fishermen raced their boats to recover his body and dropped balls of rice into the water to feed the fish so the fish would not eat him. The race and the rice balls became, over two thousand years, the dragon boat and the zongzi.端午节落在农历五月初五——公历五月底或六月初。它纪念战国诗人、楚国大夫屈原——公元前 278 年投汨罗江而亡。传说渔人划船去寻他的身躯，并把糯米团抛入江中喂鱼，让鱼别吃他的身体。划船与米团，两千年后成为龙舟与粽子。

The zongzi, and its civil war粽子，与它的"内战"

A zongzi (粽子) is a glutinous-rice dumpling wrapped in bamboo leaf, tied with reed string or kitchen twine, boiled for two to four hours until the rice is fully cooked. The shape is usually tetrahedral (a three-sided pyramid); a few regions make oblong "pillow" shapes. The leaves perfume the rice with a faint vegetal smokiness. The rice surrounds a filling. Here the trouble starts.粽子——糯米团，裹竹叶，扎芦苇条或线，煮两到四小时直到米透。形状多半是四面体（三棱锥），少数地方做长方"枕头"形。竹叶给糯米一缕淡淡的青气与烟味。米里包馅——麻烦就从这儿开始。

North of the Yangtze: sweet. Filling of red bean paste, jujube date, lotus seed paste, or just sugar. Eaten warm or cold, often with extra sugar at the table. The Beijing classic is a small zongzi with three or four jujubes and nothing else.长江以北： 甜口。馅是红豆沙、红枣、莲蓉、或只放糖。温热或冷食，吃时再加白糖。北京经典是小粽子里塞三四颗红枣，别的不放。

South of the Yangtze: savory. The Jiaxing zongzi is the canonical southern version: glutinous rice marinated in soy and shaoxing wine, wrapped around a thick chunk of fatty pork that has been pre-cured in soy for a day. Boiled for three hours, the pork melts into the rice. A Cantonese zongzi (裹蒸粽) takes this further: salted egg yolk, mung bean, chestnut, dried shrimp, Chinese sausage, mushroom, peanuts — sometimes weighing 800 grams a piece.长江以南： 咸口。嘉兴粽是南方经典：糯米先以酱油、绍兴酒腌过，裹一块预先酱腌一天的肥猪肉。煮三小时，猪肉化进米里。粤式裹蒸粽更狠：咸蛋黄、绿豆、栗子、虾米、腊肠、香菇、花生——一只可达 800 克。

A 2024 informal survey of 8,000 Chinese internet users on whether sweet or savory zongzi was "correct" produced a near-perfect 49–51 north-south split, with the Yangtze acting as the dividing line so precisely that you could draw it on a map. The argument continues annually. We do not take sides; both are correct.2024 年在 8,000 名中国网民里做过一份"哪种粽子才正宗"的非正式调查，结果近乎完美的 49 比 51，长江精确地成为分界——在地图上可以画出来。这场争论年年继续。我们不站队；两边都对。

Five-yellow at noon中午的"五黄"

In Jiangnan, a more complete Duanwu lunch is built around "the five yellows" (五黄) — five foods named for or containing the color yellow, eaten at midday on the fifth day. The five vary by household but typically include: yellow rice wine (黄酒, Shaoxing), yellow croaker (黄鱼), salted duck egg with the brilliantly orange yolk (咸鸭蛋), yellow cucumber (黄瓜), and yellow eel (黄鳝). The pairing is medicinal as much as gastronomic: the early summer is, in traditional Chinese medicine, the season when "five poisons" (snake, scorpion, centipede, gecko, toad) are most active, and the yellow foods are meant to push the body's qi back toward balance.在江南，端午中午的更完整版本是"五黄"——五样以"黄"为名或带黄色的食物，五月初五正午食。各家略有不同，常见的是：黄酒（绍兴）、黄鱼、咸鸭蛋（黄澄澄的蛋黄）、黄瓜、黄鳝。这套搭配的逻辑既是医食同源：传统中医里，初夏是"五毒"（蛇蝎蜈蚣壁虎蟾蜍）最盛的季节，吃黄物以正人体之气。

Realgar wine — almost lost雄黄酒 — 几近消失

A traditional Duanwu drink — realgar wine (雄黄酒), shaoxing wine infused with a small amount of mineral realgar — was historically poured around door frames to keep evil spirits and insects out, and dabbed on children's foreheads in the character "王" (king). The custom has mostly faded; modern toxicology has correctly identified realgar (arsenic sulfide) as poisonous, and the wine is no longer drunk. The custom of dabbing the symbol survives in some rural Jiangnan villages, with safer red pigment substituted.端午的传统饮品——雄黄酒（绍兴酒里加少量雄黄）——历史上用来沿门框洒、防虫驱邪，还会蘸一点在小孩额头写"王"字。这一习俗大多淡了；现代毒理学正确指出雄黄（硫化砷）有毒，酒已不再饮。在江南某些乡村，写"王"字的仪式还在，但改用更安全的红颜料。

The dragon boat race划龙舟

In southern cities — Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Fuzhou, Hangzhou — the dragon boat race is a serious sporting event. Slim 12–20 person canoes painted with dragon heads, paddled in unison to a drumbeat, are raced on rivers, lakes, and harbors. Boat-building, paddler training, and team practice run for months before. The international dragon boat racing federation now counts member countries on three continents. The festival has, in this sense, exported the most cinematic of its three components — the boat, not the food — and the food remains the more domestic, more enduring part.在南方城市——香港、广州、福州、杭州——划龙舟是认真的体育赛事。12 到 20 人窄船，船头雕龙首彩绘，在鼓点里齐桨而行，在江、湖、港里竞渡。造船、训练、合练要花几个月。国际龙舟联合会的成员国如今在三个洲。从这意义上说，端午把三件事里最具影像感的——船——出口给了世界；食物则更家庭、更持久。
