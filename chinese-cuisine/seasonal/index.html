Angle E · Seasonal节令

A year, edible.把一年做成可以吃的

In Chinese tradition, the calendar is not abstract. It is rice cakes in spring, tea in summer, mooncakes in autumn, dumplings in deep winter. The festival table is the calendar made edible.在中国传统里，日历不是抽象的。它是春天的青团、夏天的茶、秋天的月饼、隆冬的饺子。节令食物，是把日历做成了可以吃的样子。

The frame序

Two calendars, one table.两套日历，一桌饭

China keeps two calendars in its kitchen. One is the lunar calendar of festivals — Spring Festival, Qingming, Duanwu, Zhongqiu — moving against the Gregorian year. The other is the 24 solar terms (二十四节气), a strictly astronomical map of the agricultural year that has guided Chinese eating for two millennia. The festivals tell you what to celebrate. The solar terms tell you what is in the ground.中国的厨房里挂着两份日历。一份是节日的农历——春节、清明、端午、中秋——随阴历漂移。另一份是二十四节气，按太阳走的农事地图，指导中国人怎么吃了两千年。节日告诉你庆祝什么；节气告诉你地里现在长什么。

The argument for slow eating为什么节令仍然重要

Refrigeration broke the year.冰箱把"年"打碎了

A century ago, a Cantonese kitchen in March ate what Cantonese soil offered in March. Today, that same kitchen can buy New Zealand lamb in June and Chilean cherries in February. Refrigeration, container shipping, and the global cold chain dissolved the agricultural calendar. The seasonal meals — Qingming greens, Duanwu zongzi, the autumn mooncake — are some of the last places where the old year still survives. They are also among the most beautiful eating in the world. We argue, gently, for keeping them.一百年前，三月的粤式厨房吃的就是三月广东的土地能给出的东西。今天同一间厨房可以六月买新西兰羊腿、二月买智利樱桃。冰箱、集装箱、全球冷链把农事日历溶掉了。而清明的青、端午的粽、秋天的月——是为数不多还把"旧年"留着的地方。它们也恰好是世界上最美的几顿饭之一。我们温和地主张：留住它们。
