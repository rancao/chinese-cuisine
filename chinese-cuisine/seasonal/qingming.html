Qingming · the cold-food festival清明 · 寒食

The fifth of April. Tombs are swept; mugwort is gathered; small green rice cakes are made by hand. A festival of remembrance, eaten outside, cold.四月五日前后。扫墓，采艾草，亲手做青团。一个为追思而存在的节，冷食，在外吃。

Qingming (清明) is one of the few Chinese festivals that follows the solar calendar rather than the lunar — it falls on the 15th day after the spring equinox, which is the 4th or 5th of April. It is two things at once: a "tomb-sweeping day" when families clean ancestral graves, burn paper money, and lay food offerings; and a "cold-food festival" (寒食节) when, by custom dating to the 7th century, no cooking fire is used and meals are taken cold, often outdoors during the family outing that follows the grave visit. The folkloric reason involves a Spring-and-Autumn-period minister named Jie Zitui who died in a forest fire while refusing court office; the historical reason is more prosaic — early April in the Yellow River basin is the season when warm weather first makes outdoor activity comfortable, and the festival absorbed a much older spring outing tradition.清明是中国少数几个按阳历过的节——春分后第十五日，多落在四月四日或五日。它同时是两件事：祭祖扫墓的日子（清家先坟、烧纸钱、摆供品）；也是"寒食节"（按沿袭自公元七世纪的旧俗不动炊火，吃冷食，常常在扫墓后的踏青里现场吃完）。民间渊源涉及春秋时期一位拒受官职、最终死于林火的介子推；历史渊源更朴素——黄河流域的四月初是春末第一波适合户外活动的时段，节日吸收了更古老的春日出游传统。

Qing tuan — the green rice ball青团 — 春天那一只

The defining food of Qingming is qing tuan (青团) — a small fist-sized ball of glutinous rice flour, kneaded with the juice of crushed mugwort (艾草) or barley grass to turn it deep green, filled with sweetened red bean paste, steamed until the wrapper turns translucent and faintly fragrant. The leaves are harvested fresh — only the soft top growth — in late March; by mid-April the plant is too tough to use. A serious household makes qing tuan from scratch in the week before Qingming, freezes half, and eats the rest cold during the family outing that follows the grave visit.清明的代表食物是青团——拳头大的糯米粉团，揉进新鲜艾草（或大麦草）的汁，呈深翠绿色，包甜豆沙馅，蒸到皮微透、隐隐带香。艾草要新——只取嫩尖——三月下旬采；到四月中艾就老了，做不出。讲究的人家在清明前一周自己做，冻一半，剩下的留扫墓后踏青时冷食。

In the last decade Shanghai bakeries have begun making qing tuan with savoury fillings — salted egg yolk and pork floss, mala beef, durian, even truffle — to widespread debate. The traditional bean-paste version still outsells all of them combined. A modest bakery on Nanjing Road sold 800,000 qing tuan in the two weeks before Qingming 2024.过去十年，上海的烘焙铺开始做咸口青团——咸蛋黄肉松、麻辣牛肉、榴莲、甚至松露——讨论很热。但传统豆沙版本的销量仍然超过其他所有口味的总和。南京路上一家不大的铺子，在 2024 年清明前两周卖了 80 万只青团。

The cold table冷桌

A traditional Qingming meal — eaten outside, often on a hillside near the family tomb, sometimes simply at a picnic table during an afternoon spring outing — is built around foods that are still good cold. The canonical Jiangnan repertoire:传统清明餐——在外吃、常在祖坟附近的山坡上，或下午踏青时随便铺一块布——围绕"冷也好吃"的食物搭建。江南的经典清单：

  • Qing tuan, two or three each.青团，每人两三只。
  • San zi (馓子) — bundles of deep-fried, salt-flavored noodles, eaten as a brittle snack.馓子——咸口炸面条小束，脆。
  • Cold smoked fish (熏鱼) — a Jiangnan classic: small carp pieces marinated in soy and five-spice, deep-fried, then dipped in a sweet sauce. Eaten cold the day after.冷熏鱼——江南经典：草鱼或青鱼切块，五香酱油腌后炸，蘸甜汁。当日做，次日冷吃。
  • Drunken chicken (醉鸡) — Shaoxing wine, salt, ginger, white pepper. Served cold, sliced.醉鸡——绍兴酒、盐、姜、白胡椒。冷切片。
  • Spring vegetables, cold-dressed. Pea shoots, mustard greens, mountain bracken — all spring tender, dressed with sesame oil and salt.冷拌春菜——豌豆苗、雪里红、蕨菜——春日嫩芽，麻油盐拌。
  • Tea eggs (茶叶蛋) — eggs steeped overnight in tea, soy, and five-spice. The traditional picnic protein.茶叶蛋——茶水、生抽、五香料里浸一夜。传统郊游蛋白。

Regional variations地方版本

Qingming food shifts by latitude.清明的食物随纬度变化。

  • Jiangnan: qing tuan supreme.江南： 青团为王。
  • Cantonese: ai bing (艾粄) — a slightly different mugwort cake with red bean and peanut, denser than qing tuan.粤地： 艾粄——艾草版本不同，配红豆与花生，比青团密实。
  • Sichuan: qing ming guo (清明粿) — the local green dumpling, often with pork and bamboo shoot filling, savoury rather than sweet.四川： 清明粿——本地版的绿团，常包猪肉笋丁，咸口。
  • Shaanxi: Qingming pancakes (清明饼) — wheat pancakes layered with scallion, eaten cold.陕西： 清明饼——多层葱花麦饼，冷食。
  • Hakka: bao zi (粄子) — a wider category of mugwort cakes with regional fillings.客家： 粄子——艾粄的更大家族，馅按地方走。

Qingming wine — the meaning of the cup清明酒 — 那杯酒

At a family tomb on Qingming, a small cup of rice wine is poured — half is placed on the grave, half is drunk by the most senior living family member. The wine is, by tradition, a sweet, mildly alcoholic spring rice wine made specifically for the festival; in Jiangnan a Shaoxing aged hua diao (花雕) is acceptable. The act is small and quiet. Around it, families eat qing tuan and tell each other stories about the ancestor being remembered. The festival is the rare Chinese ritual where the food is in the service of the memory rather than the celebration.清明在祖坟前要倒一杯酒——一半敬墓，一半由家中长辈饮下。按传统，这杯酒是专为此节酿的甜口微酒精春米酒；江南也接受陈年绍兴花雕。动作小而静。围着它，全家吃青团，互相讲被追思的那位长辈的故事。清明是中国少数几个仪式里——食物为"记得"服务，而不是为"庆祝"服务。
