Mid-Autumn · the moon meal中秋 · 团圆

The fullest moon of the year. Families eat outside, look up, and quarrel about whether a real mooncake is Cantonese lotus paste, Suzhou flaky pork, or Yunnan ham.一年里最圆的月亮。家人在户外吃饭、抬头、并就"真月饼"该是粤式莲蓉、苏式酥皮鲜肉、还是云南火腿月饼争执不下。

The Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋节) falls on the fifteenth day of the eighth lunar month — usually in September. It is the second-most-important festival in the Chinese calendar, behind only Spring Festival, and the only one centered around looking up. The moon on the fifteenth of the eighth lunar month is, by tradition, the year's fullest and brightest. Families gather outside after dinner, watch the moon, eat mooncakes, and tell the children the story of Chang'e, who drank the elixir of immortality and rose to live on the moon with her rabbit.中秋节落在农历八月十五——多在公历九月。它是中国日历里第二大节，仅次于春节，也是唯一一个以"抬头"为中心的节。八月十五的月亮按传统是一年里最圆最亮的。家人晚饭后聚到户外，看月、吃月饼、给孩子讲嫦娥的故事——她喝了不老药，飞上月亮，与玉兔同住。

The mooncake — a small, dense, expensive thing月饼 — 小、密、贵

A mooncake (月饼) is a dense, palm-sized pastry. Three traditional schools dominate, and their partisans do not yield.月饼是手掌大小、密实的点心。有三大传统派别，各家粉丝互不退让。

1 · Cantonese (广式月饼)一 · 广式

The most internationally familiar style. A tender, slightly chewy wheat-and-lard pastry encloses a thick, smooth filling of lotus-seed paste and one or two salted egg yolks. The exterior is glazed with egg wash for a deep mahogany finish and stamped with the manufacturer's name. The traditional fillings are lotus paste (莲蓉), white lotus paste (白莲蓉), red bean paste, and the elaborate "five-nut" (五仁) — almond, walnut, sesame, melon seed, pumpkin seed in a sweet honey-bound matrix. A single salted yolk represents one moon; a double yolk represents two moons, twice the wish. A Cantonese mooncake should be aged five to seven days after baking before eating, to allow the oils to bloom through the pastry.国际上最熟悉的一派。柔韧带嚼的麦面猪油皮，里面是厚实、丝滑的莲蓉与一到两枚咸蛋黄。表面刷蛋液烤出深红栗色，印上字号印记。传统馅是莲蓉、白莲蓉、豆沙、以及繁复的"五仁"——杏仁、核桃、芝麻、瓜子、南瓜子，蜜糖粘成块。一只咸蛋黄象征一轮月；双黄是两轮，愿望加倍。粤式月饼烤后须放五到七天再吃——让油从馅渗进皮，俗称"回油"。

2 · Suzhou (苏式月饼)二 · 苏式

A wildly different animal. The Suzhou mooncake is a flaky, layered, lard-shortened pastry — closer to a French croissant in technique than to its Cantonese cousin — wrapped around a savory or sweet filling. The defining Suzhou mooncake is fresh pork (鲜肉月饼) — minced pork seasoned with soy, ginger, and scallion, encased in 36 fragile flake layers, and eaten warm, ideally within twenty minutes of leaving the oven. Outside Suzhou the dish is barely known. Inside Suzhou, queues at a good shop in the week before Mid-Autumn run to two hours.完全是另一种东西。苏式月饼是猪油起酥的层皮——技术上更接近法式可颂，而非粤式月饼——包甜或咸的馅。苏式标志是鲜肉月饼——猪肉糜以酱油、姜、葱调味，包进 36 层薄如纸的酥皮，温热出炉，最好在出炉二十分钟内吃完。苏州城外几乎没人知道这道。苏州城里，中秋前一周好店门口排两小时是常态。

3 · Yunnan ham (云腿月饼)三 · 云腿月饼

A regional underdog that punches above its weight. The Yunnan mooncake is a flaky lard-pastry shell — similar to the Suzhou format — wrapped around chopped Xuanwei ham, honey, lard, sugar, and a small amount of wheat flour. Sweet, salty, faintly smoky from the ham. The dish has no equivalent in the Cantonese tradition; the Cantonese, on encountering it, treat it as exotic. Yunnan kitchens make the case that Mid-Autumn's "wholeness" applies as much to the savory pleasures of a year's harvest as to lotus seed and yolk. We agree, on the days we are in Yunnan.地域上的"以小博大"。云腿月饼是猪油起酥皮——与苏式形似——里头是切丁的宣威火腿、蜂蜜、猪油、糖与少量面粉。甜中带咸，火腿带一缕烟。这道菜在粤式传统里没有对应；粤人初尝多视之为"异域"。云南人会说，中秋的"团圆"不光属于莲蓉与蛋黄，也属于一年丰收里那点咸鲜。在云南的那几天我们是同意的。

The modern: snow skin & beyond近代：冰皮 与之后

In the 1980s Hong Kong, a refrigerated "snow skin" mooncake (冰皮月饼) appeared: an unbaked glutinous-rice-flour skin, served chilled, often dyed and filled with non-traditional fillings — durian, mango, matcha, custard, ice cream. The dish was controversial at first and is now a fixture. In the 2010s and 2020s a wave of high-end novelty mooncakes appeared from luxury hotels: black truffle, Iberian ham, foie gras, single-malt-whisky chocolate. These are marketed and gifted but not eaten in serious quantity. Most families, asked, still want lotus paste with one yolk.1980 年代香港出现冷藏的冰皮月饼——不烤的糯米粉皮，冷食，常染色，馅多非传统——榴莲、芒果、抹茶、奶黄、冰淇淋。一开始有争议，今天已成定例。2010 年代到 2020 年代，奢华酒店推出高端新奇月饼：黑松露、伊比利亚火腿、鹅肝、单一麦芽威士忌巧克力。这些主要用来送礼，认真吃的人不多。大多数家庭被问，仍然想要莲蓉单黄。

The full meal — not just the cake整顿饭 — 不只是月饼

Mid-Autumn is not "the festival of the cake." It is a full dinner with the cake as dessert and centerpiece. The traditional Mid-Autumn dishes vary by region but converge on a few themes: river crab (the Yangcheng Lake hairy crab is at peak season), pomelo (柚子 — the character sounds like "again" or "blessing"), seasonal greens, a slow-braised duck, and the cake at the end. The meal is meant to be eaten in the open air — courtyard, garden, balcony — and the family does not retire indoors until the moon has visibly risen.中秋不是"月饼节"。它是一顿完整的晚餐，月饼是甜点与压轴。各地的中秋菜不同，但围绕几件事汇合：河蟹（阳澄湖大闸蟹正值最佳）、柚子（音近"佑"）、应季青菜、慢炖鸭、最后才是月饼。这顿饭按传统要在户外吃——院子、花园、阳台——月亮升起来之前家人不进屋。

The mooncake as a political object月饼作为政治物件

A bit of history. In the late Yuan dynasty, the Han uprising that eventually overthrew Mongol rule (and founded the Ming) used mooncakes as a covert means of communication: small slips of paper bearing instructions for a coordinated attack on a specific date — the fifteenth of the eighth lunar month — were hidden inside the cakes and distributed through bakeries. The story is partly historical, partly folklore, fully repeated to every Chinese child at some point. It is one reason the mooncake feels, even to people who do not believe the legend, slightly weighted.一段历史。元朝末年，最终推翻蒙古统治、建立明朝的汉人起义，曾用月饼做秘密通讯：写着"在八月十五一起动手"的小纸条藏在饼里，通过烘焙铺分发。这件事一半史实一半民间，所有中国孩子在某个时点都被讲过。这也是为什么——即便不信这传说的人——咬开月饼时也会觉得它略带些分量。
