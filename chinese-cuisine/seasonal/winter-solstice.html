Dongzhi · the winter solstice冬至

"Dongzhi as big as the new year." Dumplings in the north, tang yuan in the south, mutton soup almost everywhere. The longest night of the year is also the warmest meal."冬至大如年。"北方吃饺子，南方吃汤圆，几乎全国都有羊汤。一年里最长的夜，也是最暖的一餐。

The winter solstice — Dongzhi (冬至) — falls on or near December 21st each year. In the traditional Chinese understanding it is the moment when yang energy, having reached its lowest point, begins to recover; the calendar's longest night is also the turn back toward warmth. The classical saying "冬至大如年" — "Dongzhi as big as the new year" — captures the festival's old weight. In some southern villages the solstice meal is still treated as a family obligation on par with the Spring Festival reunion dinner.冬至——每年公历 12 月 21 日前后。按传统理解，这一天阳气衰极而始升；最长的夜，恰好是转向温暖的拐点。古谚"冬至大如年"说出它的旧分量。在某些南方乡村，冬至这顿饭至今与年夜饭并列，是必到的家庭义务。

The northern bowl — dumplings北方的那一碗 — 饺子

North of the Yangtze, Dongzhi is dumpling day. The folklore: Zhang Zhongjing, a 2nd-century physician of the Eastern Han dynasty, returning to his cold native village in winter, saw the villagers' ears turning frostbitten. He made small dough parcels stuffed with mutton and ground medicinal herbs, shaped like ears, simmered in soup, and gave them out free. The villagers recovered. The dish was thereafter called "jiao er" (娇耳, "delicate ears") — which became, over centuries, jiao zi (饺子). To this day in north China the saying runs: "if you don't eat dumplings on Dongzhi, your ears will freeze." Mostly a joke. Mostly.长江以北，冬至是饺子日。民间传说讲东汉医师张仲景——冬天回到自己寒冷的家乡，见乡邻耳朵冻伤，做了小面团里包羊肉与药材，形似耳朵，下汤煮，分发给乡邻。乡邻好了。这道东西自此名"娇耳"，几百年下来成了"饺子"。直到今天北方还说："冬至不吃饺子，耳朵会冻掉。"大半是玩笑话。大半。

The southern bowl — tang yuan南方的那一碗 — 汤圆

South of the Yangtze, Dongzhi is tang yuan day. Tang yuan (汤圆) — literally "soup balls" — are spheres of glutinous rice flour, sometimes plain and sometimes filled with sweetened black sesame paste, peanut, or red bean. They are boiled in either a sweet osmanthus syrup (for sweet versions) or a savoury broth (in some Jiangsu villages, with a small amount of fermented rice wine in the soup). The roundness is the point: round shapes signify wholeness, completion, the family gathered into one place. A Chaoshan family on Dongzhi will roll a single tang yuan for each family member by hand, sometimes adding one extra for an absent relative. No one cuts the rice ball. Bite by bite is correct.长江以南，冬至是汤圆日。汤圆——糯米粉搓的球，有素的，有包黑芝麻馅、花生馅、豆沙馅的。煮在桂花糖水里（甜版），或江苏部分乡村的咸汤里（汤里加一点酒酿）。圆是重点：圆形象征团圆、完整、家人齐聚一处。潮汕一户人家冬至会按人头每人搓一只，缺席的也搓一只。没人会咬开整颗，一口口吃，是对的。

The shared bowl — mutton soup共同的那一碗 — 羊汤

One dish runs the length of China at Dongzhi: yang rou tang (羊肉汤), a milky-white mutton broth. In Suzhou it is "mutton noodles" (羊肉面). In Shandong it is a clear, peppery mutton soup with white radish. In Henan and Anhui it is thicker, sometimes with green-onion sprinkled on top. In Shaanxi it is the hand-torn-bread version of pao mo. The unifying principle: a small bowl of intensely hot broth, eaten in the late afternoon when the daylight is failing, restores the body for the longest night. Traditional Chinese medicine treats mutton as a "warm-yang" meat — exactly what the solstice asks for.有一道菜从南到北贯穿冬至：羊肉汤。在苏州它叫"羊肉面"。在山东是清汤、白胡椒、白萝卜的羊汤。在豫皖更浓、撒葱花。在陕西它就是泡馍。统一的逻辑是：傍晚天色见暗时来一碗滚烫浓厚的汤，把身体备好，过这一年最长的夜。中医里羊肉是"温阳"之物——冬至要的正是这个。

The day after — winter strengthening冬至之后 — 进补

Dongzhi opens the period of "winter strengthening" (冬补) in the traditional Chinese health calendar. The folk principle: the body absorbs nutrients most effectively in winter, so the period from Dongzhi to the Spring Festival is treated as the year's window for restorative eating. In a Cantonese household this means a series of slow-simmered medicinal soups — black-bone chicken with goji berry, pork shank with eight medicinal herbs, sea cucumber with bird's nest. In a northern household this means heavier braises, mutton hot pots, and rounded fat-rich stews. The principle, in either grammar: in the deepest part of winter you eat as much as the body can hold, because spring is far away.冬至开启传统养生历里的"冬补"季。原理是身体在冬天最能吸收营养，所以从冬至到春节，被当作一年里"补身体"的窗口。粤地人家这意味着一系列文火慢煲——乌骨鸡配枸杞、八味药材猪手汤、海参炖燕窝。北方人家则是更重的红烧、羊肉锅、丰腴的炖菜。两种语法的共同原则：一年最深的冬里吃到身体能装的最多，因为春还远。

The poem at the end of the meal饭后的那首诗

Old Chinese almanacs include a Dongzhi poem to be read at the family table — the "九九消寒歌" (Song of the Nine-Nines, Dispelling the Cold). The poem counts the nine sets of nine days after the solstice, each pair charting a stage of recovery from winter: by the third nine the ground still cracks; by the seventh nine the wild geese return; by the ninth nine the spring plough is in the field. Reading the poem after the family meal is, in a few traditional households, still a thing. We are partial to traditions that involve poetry at dinner. We commend it.老黄历里有一首要在冬至家宴上读的诗——《九九消寒歌》。诗按每九天为一"九"数到八十一天，记录冬到春的每一阶段——三九大地仍裂，七九雁北归，九九春耕已在田里。在少数仍有传统的家庭里，饭后读这首诗的习惯还在。我们偏爱"吃饭时读诗"这类传统。我们推荐它。
