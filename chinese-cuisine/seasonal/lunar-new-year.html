Spring Festival · the reunion table春节 · 年夜饭

The most important meal in the Chinese calendar is also a piece of language. Whole fish stands for surplus; dumplings for ingots of gold; long noodles for long life. Every dish is a word.中国日历里最重要的一顿饭，也是一段话。整鱼是"有余"，饺子是元宝，长面是长寿。每道菜是一个字。

The reunion dinner of the lunar new year — called nian ye fan (年夜饭, "the eve-of-year meal") — is, depending on who counts, the world's largest annual human migration and the world's largest annual home-cooked meal. In 2025 the Chinese rail system moved over 500 million people in the two weeks before Spring Festival, almost all of them home. They came home to a table that was, in some form, the same table their grandparents ate at and their grandparents before them.农历新年的团圆饭——"年夜饭"，字面"除夕之夜的饭"——按计算口径不同，是世界上最大规模的年度人口流动，也是世界上最大规模的年度家庭烹饪事件。2025 年春节前两周，中国铁路运了五亿多人，多半回家。他们回去吃的那一桌饭，在某种形式上，是他们爷爷奶奶吃过的、他们爷爷奶奶的爷爷奶奶也吃过的同一桌。

The meal as a sentence把饭当成一段话

A Spring Festival table is structured around homophonic puns. Almost every dish doubles as a wish — a wish that works because the name of the dish, in Chinese, sounds like the name of the desired thing. The reunion dinner is the only meal of the year where the meaning of every dish is as important as the taste. We list the canonical six.年夜饭的结构是建立在谐音上的。几乎每道菜同时是一句祝愿——它能成立，是因为菜名的发音与那个被愿的"事"很像。一年里只有这一顿饭，每道菜的"意思"与它的"味道"同等要紧。下面是经典六道。

1 · Whole fish — 鱼 yú = 余 yú一 · 整鱼 — 鱼 = 余

A whole steamed fish, served head-on. The head should face the most senior person at the table. The character for "fish" (鱼) is pronounced identically to the character for "surplus / remainder" (余). The wish: "年年有余" — "every year may there be surplus." The fish must be eaten with at least some left over (leftovers being the literal embodiment of "surplus"). In the south, sea bass or grouper; in the north, often a freshwater carp.一条整鱼，蒸——带头。鱼头要对着桌上最长辈的人。"鱼"字与"余"字同音。祝愿是"年年有余"。鱼一定要剩一些下来——剩下的，字面就是"余"。南方多石斑、鲈鱼，北方常用鲤鱼。

2 · Dumplings — 饺子 jiǎozi, shaped like ingots二 · 饺子 — 像元宝

In northern China, the Spring Festival meal is incomplete without jiaozi — pork-and-cabbage dumplings folded by hand. The shape evokes a yuanbao, the boat-shaped ingot of gold currency used in late imperial China. Eating dumplings is symbolically taking ingots into the body. By long tradition, one dumpling in every batch contains a single coin (or a peanut, in clean modern versions); the diner who bites it has the year's luck. Some northern families make the dumplings at midnight on the eve, hands working together, conversation soft.北方的年夜饭没有饺子不行——猪肉白菜馅，全家手包。形状像元宝——晚清以前的船形金锭。吃饺子，在象征上就是吞元宝。按老传统，每锅里都有一只藏着一枚硬币（现代版改用花生），咬到的人得一年好运。一些北方家庭会在除夕子夜动手包，几双手同时干活，话压低。

3 · Long noodles — 长寿面三 · 长寿面

In the south, a single long noodle — unbroken from bowl to mouth — symbolizes long life. The noodle must not be cut. It is bad form, on Spring Festival, to bite a noodle through; you slurp it whole, as it shortens. Topped with a single egg, vegetable, and a slice of pork.南方的"长寿面"——从碗到嘴一根不断——象征长寿。面不能切。除夕这天，把面咬断是失礼的；要整根吸进嘴里、让它在嘴里缩短。配一只鸡蛋、一点青菜、一片肉。

4 · Nian gao — 年糕 = 年高四 · 年糕 — "年高"

A glutinous rice cake. The character for "cake" (糕) is a homophone for the character for "high" (高). The wish: "年年高升" — "every year may you rise higher." Eaten in many forms: pan-fried slices in the south, sweet cakes in Shanghai, the dense rectangular blocks of Ningbo, the brown sugar version of Guangdong.糯米糕。"糕"与"高"同音。祝愿"年年高升"。各地做法不同：南方煎片，上海甜年糕，宁波密实的方块，广东红糖年糕。

5 · Chicken whole — 鸡 jī五 · 一整只鸡 — "全家福"

A whole chicken — feet, neck, and head intact — is essential. The whole bird represents wholeness of the family. White-cut Cantonese poached chicken with ginger-scallion oil, or in the north a soy-braised version. The chicken is the only animal in Chinese symbolism whose every part is auspicious; nothing is discarded.一只整鸡——爪、颈、头俱全——必备。整鸡象征家的完整。粤式白切配姜葱油，或北方酱油烧。鸡在中国象征系统里是唯一"全身吉祥"的家禽，每一部分都要保留。

6 · Fa cai — 发菜 = 发财六 · 发菜 — "发财"

A black hair-like edible algae from inland China, paired in Cantonese cooking with dried oyster (hou si). The combined dish is "发菜蚝豉" — fa cai hou si — pronounced almost identically to "发财好事" (fā cái hǎo shì), "may you prosper and have good things." The dish is mostly Cantonese; northern families may substitute black mushrooms with a similar phonetic intent.发菜，是中国内地一种黑色丝状的可食用藻类。粤菜里与蚝豉同煮——这道"发菜蚝豉"听起来近"发财好事"。这道菜主要在粤地；北方家庭可能用木耳代替，意头一样。

The regional table地方的桌

A Spring Festival meal varies regionally:年夜饭按地方各有侧重：

  • Northern China: dumplings central. The fish is often optional. The meal may feature a copper-pot mutton hot pot.北方： 饺子为中心。鱼有时是选项。可能上一锅铜锅涮羊肉。
  • Jiangnan: a richer table with smoked fish (熏鱼), drunken chicken (醉鸡), eight-treasure rice (八宝饭), and braised pork.江南： 桌更丰——熏鱼、醉鸡、八宝饭、红烧肉。
  • Cantonese: a "poon choi" — a layered casserole of seafood, meats, dried goods — feeds the extended family from a single basin.粤地： 盆菜——海味、肉类、干货层层堆叠，全家从一只大盆里吃。
  • Sichuan: the heat is dialed up — la rou (cured pork), suan cai fish, smoked goose.四川： 辣加重——腊肉、酸菜鱼、熏鹅。
  • Chaoshan: the brine pot makes a centerpiece — a whole brine-cooked goose at the center of the table.潮汕： 卤水锅当主位——一整只卤鹅放正中央。

The night that follows饭后的那一夜

After dinner, families stay up until midnight — shou sui (守岁), "guarding the year." At midnight, fireworks (legal restrictions notwithstanding), red envelopes for the children, and a small second meal of dumplings or tang yuan. The fire stays on a low flame for a few hours after the meal because, by old superstition, an empty stove on New Year's eve invites bad luck. A serious household cooks too much on purpose so that there are leftovers — leftovers are "surplus." Surplus is the whole point.饭后，全家守到子夜——"守岁"。子夜放鞭炮（在地方法规许可下）、给孩子发红包、再吃一小顿饺子或汤圆。灶火饭后还要留一会儿小火——老说法是除夕灶冷招霉。讲究的人家故意多做几道菜，让它"剩"——剩下的就是"余"。余，是这顿饭的全部意思。
