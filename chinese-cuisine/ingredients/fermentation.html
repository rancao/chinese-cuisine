Chinese fermentation中式发酵

If French cooking is the art of sauce, Chinese cooking is the art of the fermented jar. Soy sauce, doubanjiang, rice wine, vinegar, fermented tofu, pickled mustard greens. The hidden engine of every Chinese flavor.如果说法餐是酱汁的艺术，中餐就是发酵的艺术。酱油、豆瓣酱、米酒、醋、腐乳、酸菜。这是中国味道隐形的发动机。

Every Chinese kitchen, from a Lanzhou dough station to a Hong Kong dim sum trolley, depends on liquids and pastes that were not made in that kitchen at all — they were made by microbes, in jars, somewhere else, over months or years. To understand Chinese flavor is to understand its fermentations. They are not garnishes, they are the foundation: soy sauce alone has more cultural weight in the Chinese pantry than salt does in the French one.从兰州的面案到香港的点心车，每一间中国厨房都依赖一类东西——液与酱——这类东西并不在这间厨房里完成。它们是在别处，由微生物，在缸里，用几个月到几年时间，做出来的。要懂中国味道，就要懂这些发酵。它们不是装饰，它们是地基：单是酱油在中餐里的分量，已经超过盐在法餐里的分量了。

1 · Soy sauce, the first ferment一 · 酱油，第一种发酵

Soy sauce (酱油) is a 2,500-year-old technology. The traditional process: soybeans are soaked, steamed, and inoculated with a wheat-bran starter carrying Aspergillus oryzae mold — koji (麴). The mold colonizes the beans over four to seven days, producing the enzymes that will later break soybean protein into amino acids. The mash is then submerged in brine for six to twenty-four months, during which secondary fermentation by salt-tolerant yeasts and lactic-acid bacteria builds the flavor profile. Finally the brew is pressed, settled, and pasteurized.酱油是两千五百年的技术。传统流程：黄豆泡、蒸熟、接麦麸培养的米曲霉——"麴"。四到七天里霉菌长满豆粒，分泌后续把豆蛋白分解成氨基酸的酶。豆酱再入盐水缸发酵六到二十四个月，期间耐盐酵母与乳酸菌主导二次发酵，构建风味。最后压榨、澄清、灭菌。

Chinese soy sauces come in two main families. Light soy (生抽) is the lighter, saltier, more amino-acid-forward sauce — the standard table seasoning. Dark soy (老抽) is aged longer, often with molasses added late, and is used for color and a faint caramel sweetness in braises. The differences are not interchangeable; a good kitchen has both within arm's reach. The further south you go, the lighter the soy. Cantonese kitchens prize a clear amber light soy that takes years to brew well; Northeastern kitchens lean on darker, heavier sauces that can stand up to a sauerkraut-pork stew.中国酱油分两大系。生抽更淡、更咸、氨基酸感更突出，是日常调味的基准。老抽陈酿更久，后期常加糖蜜，主要负责上色与那点焦糖甜味——多用于红烧。两者不可互替；好厨房手边两瓶并存。越往南走，酱油颜色越浅；粤菜推崇的清亮琥珀色生抽，需要几年才酿得好。东北厨房则偏深厚酱油，能与酸菜白肉那一锅平起平坐。

2 · Doubanjiang — the soul of Sichuan二 · 豆瓣酱 — 川菜之魂

If soy sauce is national, doubanjiang (豆瓣酱) is provincial — and Pixian doubanjiang, made in a small town outside Chengdu, is the gold standard. The base is broad beans rather than soy, fermented with a wheat-flour koji and chili peppers in giant unglazed clay jars exposed to weather. By tradition, the jars are turned to the sun by day and the dew by night, stirred every few mornings. Fermentation lasts at least one full summer; three years produces an entry-level "high-grade" sauce, five years is reserved for serious kitchens, and seven-year batches are catalogued and sold by the kilogram to professional chefs. We have seen old Pixian factories where the jar yards alone cover three football pitches and the air smells, faintly and pleasantly, of a dish you have not eaten yet.如果说酱油是全国性的，豆瓣酱就是省域的——而郫县豆瓣（在成都郊外一个小镇制作）是金标。底子是蚕豆而非黄豆，与小麦曲、辣椒一起入大缸露天发酵。传统是"日晒夜露"，每隔几天清晨翻一次。发酵至少经过一整夏；三年算入门"高级"豆瓣，五年是认真餐厅的库存，七年的批次编号入册，按公斤卖给职业厨师。我们去过老郫县豆瓣厂——光晒缸场就有三个足球场那么大，空气里淡淡地散着"你还没吃的那道菜"的香。

3 · Fermented tofu — furu三 · 腐乳

Fermented tofu (腐乳) is, depending on the family, either an essential condiment or a bracing acquired taste. Cubes of firm tofu are inoculated with Actinomucor or Mucor mold (similar to camembert), allowed to grow a downy coat, then submerged in brine with rice wine, salt, and spices. The yeasts and proteases convert tofu protein into glutamates, amino acids, and various aroma compounds — the result is a soft, salty, slightly funky cube with a flavor density that any Italian aged cheese would recognize. The white variety (白腐乳) is the everyday sort, sometimes blended into stir-fries. The red (红腐乳, "red curd") is pigmented with red yeast rice and used to lacquer roasted pork. The pungent stinky tofu (臭豆腐) is a more aggressive cousin.腐乳——按家庭口味——要么是必备调料，要么是需要"练"出来的口味。豆腐切方块、接放线菌或毛霉（与卡门贝尔奶酪类似），让它长出一层绒毛，再泡入米酒、盐、香料的卤水。霉菌与酵母把豆腐蛋白转成谷氨酸、氨基酸与各种风味物——结果是一颗软、咸、带一点"野"的小方块，密度任何一种意大利老熟奶酪都能理解。白腐乳是日常款，常被用来调炒菜。红腐乳被红曲染色，用于上烧肉的红光。臭豆腐则是它脾气更大的堂兄弟。

4 · Rice wine — huangjiu四 · 黄酒（米酒）

Shaoxing rice wine (绍兴黄酒) is the cooking wine of choice for almost every Chinese kitchen — and a drinking wine of considerable historical depth. It is a parallel-fermented rice beverage: a wheat-and-barley "qū" cake inoculates glutinous rice with both molds (which convert starch to sugar) and yeasts (which convert sugar to alcohol). The two processes happen simultaneously, producing a wine of 14–18% alcohol with rich amber color and the unmistakable nutty depth of an oxidized rice ferment. In cooking, a splash deglazes a wok and provides depth; aged for ten or twenty years, it is sipped at room temperature from small porcelain cups in Shaoxing's old town.绍兴黄酒，是几乎每一间中国厨房的料酒首选——同时也是一种历史悠久的饮用酒。它是"并行复式发酵"的米酒：以麦曲既接霉（把淀粉转化成糖）又接酵母（把糖转化成酒）。两套同步进行，得到 14–18 度的酒，色琥珀，带一种被氧化过的米酒特有的坚果味。下厨时，一勺淋锅边能起香增厚；陈酿十几二十年的，则在绍兴老城的瓷杯里温饮。

5 · Vinegar — the four kings五 · 醋 · 四大名醋

China codifies four major vinegar regions. Zhenjiang (镇江) in Jiangsu produces the canonical dark, fragrant rice-grain vinegar — the bottle next to a basket of soup dumplings. Shanxi mature vinegar (山西陈醋) is the heavyweight: aged for at least three years in earthenware jars exposed to summer heat and winter cold so that water slowly evaporates and the acidity concentrates. Sichuan Baoning (保宁醋) incorporates traditional Chinese herbs into the mash and is used in cold dressings and sweet-and-sour dishes. Fujian Yongchun (永春老醋) uses a red-yeast-rice koji and is sweeter, almost balsamic in profile.中国有"四大名醋"。镇江香醋，江苏出，深色香米醋，小笼旁边那一碟。山西陈醋是重型——陶缸里露天陈酿至少三年，让水分缓慢蒸发、酸度浓缩。四川保宁醋在曲里加传统中草药，用于凉拌与糖醋菜。福建永春老醋用红曲发酵，口感偏甜，接近意大利香醋的"味位"。

6 · Pickled vegetables — the everyday ferment六 · 腌菜 — 日常的发酵

Below the high-status ferments — soy sauce, doubanjiang, rice wine, vinegar — sits an enormous floor of household-scale fermentations: Sichuan pao cai (泡菜) in salt brine, Northeast suancai (酸菜) in pressed crocks, Cantonese preserved mustard greens (梅菜), Hakka aged radish (菜脯), Yunnan pickled chili (糟辣), and a dozen regional variants of pickled bamboo shoot (酸笋). The technique is mostly lactic-acid fermentation by the bacteria that naturally live on the vegetables. Salt and time do the work. Every household had its own jar; every grandmother had her own recipe. Industrial production has not replaced this, only supplemented it.高阶发酵之下——酱油、豆瓣、黄酒、醋——是一层巨大的家庭发酵：四川泡菜（盐水）、东北酸菜（压缸）、广东梅菜、客家菜脯（陈萝卜干）、云南糟辣、十几种地方版酸笋。技术多半是乳酸菌发酵——蔬菜表面本来就有这些菌，盐与时间做工。家家有一缸，奶奶各有一方。工业品没替代它，只是又添了一层。
