A cuisine is not a list of dishes.一门菜系，不是一张菜单

A cuisine is a pantry, a vocabulary of techniques, a way of holding a knife, a tolerance for fermentation, a sense for the exact temperature at which oil should accept the first ginger slice. The dishes are the visible part. Everything that makes those dishes possible lives below. These six essays go below.一门菜系是一柜食材，一套技艺词汇，一种握刀的方式，一种对发酵的耐受，一种"油可以下姜"的温度感。菜是显出来的那部分；让这些菜成立的一切，藏在下面。这六篇，写下面。

A throughline一根贯穿线

Time is an ingredient.时间是一种食材

If you read these six together, one idea repeats: the Chinese pantry treats time as a raw material. Fermentation is time. Drying is time. A century-old brine pot is time. A correctly heated wok is also time — measured in seconds rather than years, but time nonetheless. The technical depth of the cuisine is partly the willingness to wait, and partly the precision of knowing when not to.把这六篇串起来读，会反复看到同一个想法：中国食材柜把"时间"当成原材料。发酵是时间。晒干是时间。一锅传了百年的老卤是时间。一只到位的锅，也是时间——以秒为单位，但仍然是时间。这门菜的技术深度，一半在于愿意等，一半在于知道什么时候不能再等。
