Knife work刀工

A Chinese kitchen has one knife. The cleaver — the cai dao — slices, juliennes, dices, mince-pounds, splits poultry bones, scrapes the board clean, and carries the chopped pile to the wok. The size of the cut is half the dish.中国厨房只有一把刀。菜刀——它切、它丝、它丁、它剁、它劈鸡骨、它刮案板、它把切好的料铲进锅。切多大，就是这道菜的一半。

A Western kitchen has a chef's knife, a paring knife, a slicer, a boning knife, a santoku, perhaps a Chinese cleaver kept somewhere awkward. A Chinese kitchen has one knife on the cutting board. The Chinese cleaver — cai dao (菜刀) — is a rectangular blade roughly 20 cm long and 10 cm tall, weighted toward the spine, sharpened to about 15 degrees on each side. It looks brutal. In a competent hand, it is among the most agile knives ever made.西餐厨房的台面上有：主厨刀、削皮刀、片刀、剔骨刀、三德刀，可能还有把中式菜刀放在某个不方便的角落。中餐厨房的案板上只有一把刀。菜刀——长方刀身，约 20 厘米长 10 厘米高，重心偏刀背，每面磨大约 15 度。看着粗暴。会用的人手里，它是有史以来最灵活的刀之一。

1 · One blade, every job一 · 一刀百用

The cai dao's range comes from how you hold it. A pinch grip on the blade itself — thumb and index on the metal, three fingers on the handle — gives precise control for thin slicing. The wide flat of the blade smashes a clove of garlic with one slap. The heel splits a chicken bone. The spine taps a clove free of skin. The flat side scoops chopped material into the wok. The point — used surprisingly often — picks a single seed out of a pepper. One knife, twelve jobs.菜刀的功用面广，关键在握法。拇指与食指捏刀身、三指握刀柄——这是"夹握"，做薄切时给极精的控制。刀身宽面"啪"一下拍裂一瓣蒜。刀跟劈鸡骨。刀背敲蒜让皮自动脱落。刀身侧面把切好的料铲进锅。刀尖——其实用得也不少——挑掉一粒椒籽。一把刀，十二种活。

2 · The seven canonical cuts二 · 七种基本刀法

A Chinese culinary school teaches seven fundamental cuts:中餐烹饪学校教七种基本刀法：

  • Pian (片) — slicing. A flat blade goes through the ingredient at a slight angle to the board.片——将食材切成薄片。刀面与案板呈小角。
  • Si (丝) — julienne, into matchstick strips. Each si should be uniform in cross-section.丝——切成火柴梗细的丝。每根截面要一致。
  • Ding (丁) — small cubes, typically 1 cm. Used in kung pao chicken, mapo tofu.丁——一厘米左右的小方块。宫保鸡丁、麻婆豆腐用。
  • Tiao (条) — batons, larger than si.条——比丝大的长条。
  • Mo / Rong (末 / 茸) — mince, then paste. Done with a rocking action of the cleaver.末 / 茸——剁到末、再剁到茸。摇刀动作。
  • Kuai (块) — irregular chunks, often "rolling-knife" cut where you turn the ingredient between cuts to expose new faces.块——不规则块，常用"滚刀"——切一刀就把食材旋转一下，露出新面。
  • Hua dao (花刀) — decorative scoring. Diagonal cuts on squid, kidney, pork loin, that open into floral shapes when cooked.花刀——装饰刀工。在鱿鱼、腰花、里脊上斜划，下锅后开成花状。

3 · Why the size matters三 · 为什么大小要紧

A stir-fry is fast — 60 to 180 seconds in a screaming-hot wok — and every ingredient must cook through in the same time window. If the carrot is cubed at 1 cm and the chicken is cubed at 5 mm, the chicken will overcook before the carrot is tender. If the noodles are wide and the noodles' garnish is fine julienne, the eater will get a long noodle and an unrelated puff of carrot rather than a coherent bite. A Chinese chef sizes ingredients to time, not to plating.炒菜是快的——猛火上 60 到 180 秒——每一样食材必须在同一段时间内熟透。如果胡萝卜切 1 厘米丁，鸡肉切 5 毫米丁，鸡肉熟透时胡萝卜还没软。如果面条是宽的，配菜却是细丝，吃的人会得到一根长面外加一阵不相干的胡萝卜屑，而不是一筷子完整的味道。中餐厨子按时间切，不按装盘切。

There is a corresponding principle about pairing: ding goes with ding, si goes with si, pian goes with pian. A great cook will, looking at the same five ingredients, make a different dish depending on which family of cuts they choose. Switch the cut, switch the dish.还有一条搭配原则：丁配丁，丝配丝，片配片。同样的五样食材，老厨子选不同的刀工"语族"，做出的菜就不同。换刀工，换一道菜。

4 · The decorative cuts — fish-fragrant kidney四 · 装饰刀工 · 鱼香腰花

The most theatrical Chinese knife work is decorative scoring — hua dao. Pork kidney is the famous training piece: the kidney is split, its inner membrane removed, then the flat side is scored in a tight grid of diagonals about 2 mm deep. When the kidney hits hot oil it curls inward at every cut, producing what looks like a small bouquet. Squid and pork loin get similar treatment. The cuts are not for show; the scored surface dramatically increases the area exposed to oil, so the seasoning sticks and the texture changes.中餐最戏剧化的刀工是装饰性的——花刀。猪腰是最经典的训练菜：腰子剖开、去内膜，平面上以约 2 毫米深、紧密的斜线打格。下热油，每一刀痕处都向里卷，看上去像一朵小花束。鱿鱼、里脊也常这么处理。这不只是为了好看——划开的表面让接触油的面积陡增，调味挂得住，口感也变了。

5 · The board五 · 案板

A serious Chinese cutting board is a thick disc cut from a single tree — often a Ginkgo biloba, occasionally a willow or a teak. Twenty centimetres thick, fifty across, weighing 15 kg, with a slight concave wear pattern from years of chopping. The wood absorbs the shock of the cleaver and is gentle on the edge. A new Chinese cook receives a board, an apron, and a cleaver from their mentor. The cleaver they sharpen themselves. The board they inherit.一块认真的中式案板，是从一棵树横切的整盘——常常是银杏，偶尔柳木或柚木。20 厘米厚，50 厘米径，15 公斤，多年的剁刀把它磨出一个微凹的弧。木头吸震、护刃。中餐学徒入门，师父给他一把刀、一条围裙、一块板。刀，他自己磨。板，他传承。
