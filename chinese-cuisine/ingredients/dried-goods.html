Dried goods干货

A Chinese kitchen treats dehydration not as a preservation tactic but as a separate ingredient class. The dried mushroom is a different ingredient from the fresh mushroom. Dried scallops are not "scallops, dehydrated." They are something else, with their own grammar.中国厨房把"干"当成一个独立的食材类，而不只是保存手段。干香菇不是"香菇的脱水版"。瑶柱不是"扇贝晾干"。它们是另一种东西，有自己的语法。

The Chinese dried-goods drawer is one of the world's great pantry concepts. The principle: drying is not a backup for fresh ingredients. Drying transforms an ingredient — concentrating its glutamates, breaking down its proteins, making available aromas that the fresh version does not have. A dried shiitake has roughly ten times the inosinate content of a fresh one. A dried scallop has 60 times the glutamate of a fresh one. The Chinese kitchen reaches for dried not when the fresh is unavailable, but when the dried's flavor is the right answer.中国干货柜是世界上最厉害的食材概念之一。逻辑是：晒干不是替代品。晒干会改变食材——浓缩它的谷氨酸、分解它的蛋白、让一些鲜品没有的香气浮现出来。干香菇的肌苷酸含量约是鲜香菇的十倍。干瑶柱的谷氨酸约是鲜扇贝的六十倍。中国厨房用干，不是因为鲜的拿不到，而是因为干的味道恰好就是答案。

1 · Dried scallop — yaozhu (瑶柱)一 · 瑶柱 · 干贝

Yaozhu — the dried adductor muscle of the conpoy scallop — is the most prestigious dried good on the Cantonese shelf. The drying takes a full month under sun and wind; the result is a hard amber pellet about the size of a fingernail that smells of the deep sea. To use it, soak in cold water overnight, then either steam the scallops whole (in xo sauce, a Cantonese hot sauce made from yaozhu, dried shrimp, ham, chili and oil), or shred them with the fingers and stir into rice congee or soup. Aroma you cannot get any other way.瑶柱——干贝、扇贝闭壳肌的干燥品——是粤式干货柜上最贵重的一种。要在日晒风干里晾足一个月；做出来是一颗指甲大的硬琥珀，闻起来是远海。用法：冷水隔夜泡发，整粒蒸（或与瑶柱、虾米、火腿、辣椒、油一起做成 XO 酱），或者用手撕成丝，下粥、下汤。换任何其他做法都得不到这种香气。

2 · Dried mushrooms — xianggu, hua gu, songrong二 · 干菌 · 香菇、花菇、松茸干

A high-quality dried shiitake — particularly the "flower-mushroom" or hua gu (花菇), a thick-capped variety with a cracked white-and-brown surface — has a meaty depth that no fresh mushroom replicates. The drying concentrates guanylate, the umami compound that makes mushrooms taste of mushrooms. A Cantonese braise of pork with hua gu is one of the cuisine's most quietly impressive dishes — three ingredients, four hours, indescribable result. Dried morel (羊肚菌) and dried matsutake (松茸干) are higher-status, but a 5-yuan dried shiitake from a Hong Kong herb shop will lift any pot of stock you put it in.高品质的干香菇——尤其是"花菇"，菌伞厚、表面有黑白裂纹的那种——风味厚到鲜香菇追不上。晾干让鸟苷酸浓缩，是"菇味"之源。粤式花菇炖猪肉，是粤菜里最低调地强大的一道——三样东西，四小时，难以形容。干羊肚菌、干松茸更贵气，但香港药材铺里 5 块钱的一只干香菇，扔进任何一锅清汤里都能立刻把汤拉高一个层次。

3 · Dried bamboo shoot — sun gan (笋干)三 · 笋干

Fresh bamboo shoots have a clean, vegetal sweetness; dried bamboo shoots have something else — a chew-y, almost smoky character that defines a particular subset of Hangzhou, Anhui, and Fujian dishes. The drying involves boiling the shoots until tender, sun-drying, and then often smoking. Rehydration is slow — soak for at least 24 hours, change the water twice — and the result, in a long braise with pork belly and bean paste, is one of Jiangnan's masterpieces (笋干红烧肉).鲜笋是干净的青甜；笋干是另一种东西——耐嚼、近乎烟熏的性格，定义了一小群杭州、徽州、闽北的菜。做法是煮熟、晒干，常常再熏。泡发需要至少 24 小时，换两次水。与五花肉、豆豉同烧——笋干红烧肉——是江南一道顶级。

4 · Dried seafood — gan bei, dried shrimp, fish maw四 · 海干货 · 干贝、虾米、花胶

Dried shrimp (虾米 / 海米) is the workhorse of the dried-seafood drawer — a tablespoon thrown into a stir-fry adds depth that fresh shrimp cannot, and at a fraction of the cost. Fish maw (花胶 / 鱼肚) — the dried swim bladder of large fish — is a luxury ingredient with a gelatinous texture, used in Cantonese banquet soups. Dried oyster (蚝豉) is what its name says, and it is the secret weapon of the famous Cantonese fa cai oyster stew served at Lunar New Year. Each of these has its own soak time, its own rehydration ritual, and its own seasonality. A serious Cantonese cook knows them all by sight and by smell.虾米是干海货柜里的主力——炒菜里撒一勺，能带来鲜虾给不出的厚味，价格还便宜得多。花胶（鱼肚，即大鱼鱼鳔晒干）是高级食材，胶质重，多用于粤式宴席汤。蚝豉就是字面意思的"晒干的蚝"——是过年那道发菜蚝豉煲的秘密武器。每一种都有自己的泡发时间、自己的发法、自己的季节。认真的粤厨光看光闻就能认出来。

5 · Lap mei — cured meats of the south五 · 腊味 · 南方的风干

"Lap mei" (腊味) is the Cantonese term for an entire category of air-cured meats made between October and February, when the south is briefly dry enough. Lap cheong (腊肠) — sweet pork-and-liver sausage — is the most familiar. Lap yuk (腊肉) is cured pork belly. Lap aap (腊鸭) is wind-dried duck. All three are sold by the strip in late autumn markets, hung over the cooking fire by old kitchens, and end up in claypot rice (煲仔饭) where the rendered fat soaks into the bottom layer of rice and produces the most coveted crust in Chinese cooking.腊味是粤地的"风干肉"统称，每年十月到二月在南方相对干燥的几个月做。腊肠——带肝甜口的猪肉肠——是最熟悉的一种。腊肉是猪五花的干腊版。腊鸭是风干鸭。三样秋末市场都成串卖；老厨房会把它们挂在灶火上方。最终它们落地于煲仔饭——腊味油在最下层米里渗开，做出中餐里最金贵的那一层锅巴。

6 · A note on storage六 · 关于存放

Dried goods are durable but not eternal. A dried scallop loses serious aromatic intensity after 12–18 months. A dried shiitake holds its umami for two years if kept dry and dark; after that, it starts tasting of cardboard. Store everything in air-tight glass jars away from heat. The exception is dried tangerine peel and fish maw, both of which improve with age — five-year aged peel is more valuable than fresh; ten-year fish maw is gifted in lacquered boxes.干货耐放但不"永生"。瑶柱过 12–18 个月，香气开始衰退。干香菇在干燥、避光环境下能保 2 年；之后开始有"纸味"。一律玻璃罐密封、避热。例外是陈皮与花胶，越陈越好——五年陈皮比新鲜值钱；十年花胶以漆盒包装当礼物。
