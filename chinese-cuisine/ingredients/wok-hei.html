Wok hei锅气

"Wok hei" — the breath of the wok — is what makes a stir-fry from the alley shop taste different from a stir-fry at home. We try to write down what fire is doing."锅气"——锅里呼出来的那口气——是巷子口大排档的炒菜和家里厨房不一样的原因。我们试着把"火在干什么"写下来。

Ask a Cantonese chef what wok hei is and they will smile and shrug and say "fire" (火). Ask a food scientist and they will tell you about Maillard reactions, free-radical oxidation of oil, the volatilization of polyaromatic compounds at around 200°C, and the burning of trace organics on the inside of a well-seasoned wok. Both are correct. Wok hei sits at the intersection of physics and craft, and is the single most discussed and least translated concept in Chinese cooking.问粤厨什么是锅气，他会笑着耸耸肩说"火"。问食品科学家，他会跟你解释美拉德反应、油的自由基氧化、200°C 左右多环芳香化合物的挥发、以及一只养好的锅内壁上痕量有机物的燃烧。两边都对。锅气坐落在物理与手艺的交叉点上，是中餐里讨论最多、翻译最难的概念。

1 · What wok hei is not一 · 锅气不是什么

It is not a seasoning. You cannot add it from a bottle. It is not "smoky" in the way that grilled meat is smoky. It is not "char." It is not simply the result of a hot pan. A Western steel skillet at the same temperature, with the same oil and ingredients, will not produce wok hei. The geometry of the wok matters; the fire matters; the timing matters; and so, crucially, does the seasoning of the wok — the patina of polymerized oil and burned-on residue on the inside of a pan that has been used for years.它不是一种调料。瓶装的没有。它不像烤肉那种"烟熏"。它不是"焦"。它不只是"锅热"。一只西式不锈钢平底锅，同温度、同油、同料，做不出锅气。锅的形状要紧；火要紧；时机要紧；最关键的——锅"养"得怎么样：经年使用后内壁那层聚合油膜与碳化残渣。

2 · The recipe, sort of二 · 做法，勉强写下来

The conditions for wok hei, in approximate order:锅气的条件，约略按顺序：

  1. An adequately powerful flame. Restaurant wok burners run 100,000–200,000 BTU/hour. A home gas range is 7,000–12,000 BTU. The flame must heat the wok faster than the ingredients can cool it.火够大。 餐厅的炉头是 10–20 万 BTU/小时。家用燃气灶 7,000–12,000 BTU。火要够快地补热——比食材冷却锅的速度快。
  2. A carbon-steel wok, properly seasoned. The pan must reach 200–280°C before the oil is added. The seasoning layer (a black-brown polymer film) is essential — it carries the volatile organics that produce the "smoke" smell.一只养好的铁锅。 锅要先到 200–280°C 才下油。养出来那层黑棕色的聚合膜不可或缺——它带的痕量有机物，是"烟"味的来源。
  3. A high-smoke-point oil, used aggressively. Peanut oil, lard, refined soybean. The oil hits the hot wok, immediately smokes, and the smoke is meant to coat the food.高烟点的油，敢用。 花生油、猪油、精炼大豆油。油下到热锅，瞬间冒烟——这缕烟，正是要附在食材上的。
  4. Constant motion. The wok is tossed, not stirred. The contents are airborne for several seconds at a time, flame-licked, returned. The tossing exposes every surface to direct flame for fractions of a second, producing the controlled char.不停翻。 锅是颠的，不是搅的。食材在空中飞几秒、被火舌舔过、再落回。每一面都在毫秒级直面火苗，得到可控的"焦"。
  5. Dry ingredients. Anything wet drops the wok temperature drastically and you lose wok hei in five seconds. Patted-dry beef. Vegetables shaken to remove rinse water.干。 任何带水的料一下锅，温度骤降，锅气五秒内散光。牛肉拍干。蔬菜甩水。
  6. Speed. A great stir-fry is over in 90 seconds. Longer than that and you have stewed, not stir-fried.快。 一道好炒菜 90 秒内出锅。再长就是焖了，不是炒。

3 · The chemistry, briefly三 · 化学，简表

Food scientists have done a decent job documenting what is going on. The dominant reactions:食品科学界对这件事的记录还算清楚。主要在发生的反应：

  • Maillard. Amino acids and reducing sugars combine at ~140–165°C to produce hundreds of new aroma compounds — the roasted-meat smell.美拉德反应。 氨基酸与还原糖在 140–165°C 结合，生成几百种新香气分子——"烤肉味"的来源。
  • Caramelization. Sugars at ~165–180°C produce furans and lactones — sweet, butterscotch-like notes.焦糖化。 糖在 165–180°C 生成呋喃类与内酯——焦糖、奶油糖的香气。
  • Oil oxidation. At ~200°C, oil decomposes into pyrazines, aldehydes, and free fatty acids — the "smoky" component people associate with wok hei. This is where the seasoned wok matters most: the patina catalyzes specific oxidation pathways the bare metal does not.油的氧化。 油在 200°C 左右开始分解，产生吡嗪、醛、游离脂肪酸——人们感知为"烟味"的那一支。这里养锅最关键：那层旧膜会催化裸金属做不出的特定氧化路径。
  • Direct flame contact. When the wok is tossed, droplets of oil ignite mid-air. The flame reaches ~1,500°C briefly and produces pyrolysis products on the surface of the food — a controlled, instantaneous char.火苗直接接触。 颠锅时，飞起的油滴在空中点燃。火苗 1,500°C 左右掠过食材表面，瞬间裂解出热解产物——可控的、毫秒级的"焦"。

4 · Can you make wok hei at home?四 · 家里能不能做出锅气？

Mostly no. Honestly. A home stove cannot produce the BTUs and a domestic kitchen does not want the smoke. But a few mitigations help: get a carbon-steel wok and season it religiously, preheat empty for two full minutes, work in small batches (200 g of meat at a time), keep ingredients aggressively dry, and finish a dish by drizzling a teaspoon of shaoxing wine around the rim — the wine vaporizes instantly on contact with the metal and the resulting flash gives a domestic approximation of the volatile profile. None of this will produce restaurant-grade wok hei. But you will get 60% of the way there, which is, for most cooking, plenty.基本不能。直说吧。家用炉给不了那个 BTU，家用厨房也受不了那点烟。但有些办法能拉近距离：买一只铁锅、认真养它，空锅预热整整两分钟，分小批次（一次 200 克肉），食材保持极干，出锅前沿锅边淋一茶匙绍兴酒——酒接触金属瞬间气化产生闪火，能给到挥发风味一个家用近似。这一切都做不到大排档的锅气，但能到 60% — 对大多数做家常菜的人，已经够了。

5 · The dishes that need wok hei most五 · 最需要锅气的几道菜

  • Beef chow fun (干炒牛河) — the Cantonese acid test. Wide rice noodles, dark soy, beef. Without wok hei the dish is wet, soggy, gluey. With it, the noodles have a faint char and a smoky sheen.干炒牛河——粤菜里的"试金石"。河粉、老抽、牛肉。没锅气就是湿、烂、粘。有了，粉条带一点焦边与烟亮。
  • Stir-fried morning glory (清炒空心菜) — three ingredients, ten seconds in the wok. Either you have wok hei or you have nothing.清炒空心菜——三样东西，锅里待十秒。要么有锅气，要么什么都没有。
  • Yangzhou fried rice (扬州炒饭) — the rice should bounce in the pan, each grain coated and lightly toasted.扬州炒饭——米要在锅里弹起来，每粒都裹油、轻焙。
