The bottle · A taxonomy瓶罐 · 一份分类学

Sauces & vinegars酱与醋

A Chinese pantry door holds twelve bottles. Each has one job. Use the wrong one and the dish, while edible, is not what it should be. Here is what each one is for.中国食材柜门里放着十二只瓶子。每只一件活。用错一只，菜能吃，但不是它该是的样子。下面是每只瓶子的"工作描述"。

If the fermentation essay is about how the bottles are made, this one is about how they are used. The Chinese pantry has a clear taxonomy: a soy sauce family (light, dark, sweet, mushroom), a vinegar family (Zhenjiang, Shanxi, Sichuan, Fujian), a oyster-sauce / paste family (oyster, hoisin, sweet bean, soy bean), a chili family (chili oil, doubanjiang, fresh chili paste, fermented chili), and a few one-offs that defy classification (sesame oil, sand-tea sauce, fish sauce in the south). A serious kitchen has all of them within arm's reach, and a chef knows what each one tastes like with eyes closed.如果说发酵那篇讲的是这些瓶子怎么造出来的，这一篇讲的是它们怎么用。中国食材柜分类清晰：酱油一族（生抽、老抽、甜豉油、菇酱油）、醋一族（镇江、山西、四川、福建）、蚝油 / 酱泥一族（蚝油、海鲜酱、甜面酱、黄豆酱）、辣一族（辣椒油、豆瓣酱、鲜辣酱、糟辣椒）、还有几个不进类的单兵（麻油、沙茶酱、南方的鱼露）。认真的厨房，每一只都在伸手能及处；好厨子闭着眼能尝出哪是哪。

1 · The soy sauce family一 · 酱油族

Light soy (生抽). Salt-forward, amino-acid-bright, light brown. The everyday seasoning. Goes into dressings, stir-fries, dips. Dark soy (老抽). Aged longer, sometimes molasses-finished, opaque and viscous. The color builder for red-braising; provides a faint caramel sweetness. Use sparingly — a tablespoon is plenty for a whole pot. Sweet soy / sweet-soy paste (甜豉油). A Cantonese specialty: light soy thickened with sugar to a syrup. Drizzled over rice rolls (cheung fun), white-cut chicken, congee. Mushroom soy (草菇老抽). A dark soy enriched with straw-mushroom extract; doubles the umami. Common in Cantonese stir-fries.生抽。 咸味突出，氨基酸香明显，浅褐色。日常调味——拌、炒、蘸。 老抽。 陈酿更久，常加糖蜜，浓稠深褐。红烧上色用，带一点焦糖甜。少用——一锅一汤匙就够。 甜豉油。 粤地专利：生抽加糖熬到糖浆。淋肠粉、白切鸡、粥用。 草菇老抽。 老抽加草菇提取，鲜味翻倍。粤式炒菜常用。

2 · The four great vinegars二 · 四大名醋

Zhenjiang (镇江香醋). Made from glutinous rice in Jiangsu, dark brown, fragrant, rounded acidity. The vinegar for dipping soup dumplings; also for sweet-and-sour preparations. Shanxi mature vinegar (山西陈醋). Sorghum-based, aged 3–10 years outdoors, sharper and more astringent than Zhenjiang. The Shanxi household drink-vinegar; a tablespoon in a bowl of plain noodles is a meal. Sichuan Baoning (保宁醋). Made with Chinese herbal additions, rounded and slightly sweet. Used in Sichuan cold dressings and the "lychee profile" sweet-and-sour. Fujian Yongchun (永春老醋). Red-yeast-rice fermented, almost balsamic; complex and faintly sweet. The dip for Fujian fishball soup.镇江香醋。 江苏糯米酿，深褐、芳香、酸度圆润。蘸小笼用；也用于糖醋类。 山西陈醋。 高粱底，露天陈酿 3–10 年，酸度更冲、收口更紧。山西人家"喝"的醋；一勺淋一碗白面就是一餐。 四川保宁醋。 加中药材发酵，圆润微甜。川菜凉拌与"荔枝味"糖醋用。 福建永春老醋。 红曲发酵，近似意大利香醋，复杂微甜。福建鱼丸汤的蘸料。

3 · Oyster sauce, hoisin, and friends三 · 蚝油、海鲜酱及它们的兄弟

Oyster sauce (蚝油). Invented in 1888 in Guangdong by accident — a pot of simmering oysters was forgotten too long, and the resulting reduction became a sauce. Today made by simmering oysters in their own liquor with soy and caramel. Adds dark, deep, slightly briny umami. Use sparingly. Hoisin sauce (海鲜酱). "Sea-fresh sauce" — the name is misleading; there is no seafood in it. Hoisin is a sweet-savory paste of fermented soybean, sugar, vinegar, garlic, sesame, and chili. The dipping sauce for Peking duck. Sweet bean sauce (甜面酱). The base for Beijing zha jiang noodles. A toasted wheat-and-soybean paste, deeply caramel and slightly bitter. Yellow bean sauce (黄豆酱). The savory, less-sweet cousin; the workhorse of Northeast and Shandong cooking.蚝油。 1888 年广东人无意中发明——一锅煮蚝忘了关火，浓缩液成了酱。今天做法是把蚝在自身原液里慢炖，加酱油与焦糖。提供深、厚、微咸的鲜。少用。 海鲜酱。 名字误导人——里面没有海鲜。它是发酵黄豆、糖、醋、蒜、芝麻、辣椒混成的甜咸酱。北京烤鸭蘸料。 甜面酱。 北京炸酱面的底子。焙制小麦与黄豆的酱，焦糖味重，略带苦。 黄豆酱。 它咸味较重、甜味较少的兄弟；东北与鲁菜的主力。

4 · The chili family四 · 辣椒一族

Chili oil (辣椒油). Crushed dried chili steeped in hot oil with spices (star anise, cassia, peppercorn). Each kitchen has its own house version. Pixian doubanjiang (郫县豆瓣酱). Already covered in the fermentation essay; the soul of Sichuan. Pickled chili (泡椒). A Sichuan ferment — chilies in salt brine for months. The base of the "fish-fragrant" flavor profile. Fresh chili paste (鲜辣酱). Hunan's contribution: raw chili pounded with garlic, salt, fermented black bean. Sharper, brighter, less complex than doubanjiang.辣椒油。 干辣椒碎与香料（八角、桂皮、花椒）在热油里浸出。家家自己一锅。 郫县豆瓣酱。 发酵篇里讲过——川菜的魂。 泡椒。 四川的发酵——鲜椒在盐水里泡数月。鱼香味型的底。 鲜辣酱。 湘菜的招牌：生辣椒与蒜、盐、豆豉一起舂烂。比豆瓣更锐、更亮，但没那么复杂。

5 · The non-classifiable single bottles五 · 不入类的单兵

Sesame oil (麻油). Toasted sesame, cold-pressed. A garnish, not a cooking oil — added at the end. A drop on top of a clear soup transforms it. Sand-tea sauce (沙茶酱). Chaoshan's contribution, originating in Southeast Asia. A peanut, shrimp, garlic, chili, spice paste. The only dipping sauce for Chaoshan beef hot pot. Fish sauce (鱼露). Used in Fujian, Chaoshan, and parts of Cantonese cooking. A southern parallel to soy sauce, made from fermented anchovies and salt. Sweet rice wine (米酒酿). Not a sauce per se, but a key flavoring — a slightly alcoholic, sweet rice ferment used in soups, desserts, and as a marinade.麻油。 焙过的芝麻冷榨。是装点，不是炒油——出锅前才下。清汤上滴一滴就脱胎换骨。 沙茶酱。 潮汕的招牌，源自东南亚。花生、虾、蒜、辣椒、香料做成的酱。潮汕牛肉火锅唯一的蘸料。 鱼露。 福建、潮汕和部分粤菜用。南方的酱油兄弟，鳀鱼与盐发酵而成。 米酒酿（醪糟）。 严格说不是酱，但常作调味——微酒精的甜米发酵，下汤、下糖水、做腌料用。
