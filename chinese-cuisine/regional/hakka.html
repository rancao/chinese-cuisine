Hakka · 客家

The cuisine of a people who have moved, on average, every four generations for the last fifteen hundred years. Stews that travel. Salt that preserves. A kitchen that learned to be portable.一群在过去一千五百年里平均每四代搬一次家的人，做出来的菜。能远行的炖锅。能保存的盐渍。一间学会了"可携带"的厨房。

"Hakka" (客家, literally "guest families") is not a province. It is an ethnolinguistic group, descendants of Han Chinese who fled successive waves of war and dynastic collapse, moving south from the Yellow River basin starting in the 4th century. They settled, were pushed on, settled again — in Jiangxi, Fujian, Guangdong, and eventually Taiwan, Borneo, and the diaspora. Wherever they went, the food came with them: a cuisine engineered for movement, scarcity, and the long road."客家"——字面意思是"客居的家族"——不是一个省。它是一个族群，是从战乱与王朝崩塌中一波又一波向南迁徙的汉人后裔，从公元四世纪起从黄河流域一路南下。他们落脚、被赶、再落脚——江西、福建、广东，最后到台湾、婆罗洲、和更远的海外。无论走到哪里，菜跟到哪里：一门为迁徙、为匮乏、为长路而生的菜。

1 · Salt and time — the road food一 · 盐与时间 — 路上的食物

Hakka cooking is famously salty and famously preserved. The reason is migratory: salt was the cheapest, most portable way to keep meat edible across mountain passes. Salt-baked chicken (盐焗鸡), the most-cited Hakka dish, is the museum piece of this logic. A whole chicken, wrapped in paper, packed entirely inside a wok of hot coarse salt and roasted for an hour. The salt cooks the bird gently, perfumes the skin, and would (in the older usage) preserve the meat for several days of travel.客家菜出名的咸、出名的腌。原因是迁徙——盐，是把肉跨越山隘还能吃的最便宜、最便携的方式。盐焗鸡，最常被引用的客家菜，是这套逻辑的博物馆藏品：整鸡，纸包，埋进一锅烧热的粗盐里焗一小时。盐温柔地把鸡焗熟，让皮带着咸香——并在更古老的用法里，让肉在路上能多撑几天。

Other examples of salt-and-time: preserved mustard greens (梅菜) — the famous companion of Dongpo pork in its Hakka variant 梅菜扣肉; preserved radish (菜脯), aged for years in earthen jars; jiu cai (酒糟) pork, ribs cured in rice-wine lees; and a whole genre of salt-preserved pork bellies hung over the winter cooking fire.盐与时间的其他例子：梅菜——客家版梅菜扣肉的灵魂；菜脯（陈年萝卜干），土陶罐里能存数年；酒糟排骨；以及一整类用米酒糟腌过、悬在冬日灶火上熏成的腊肉。

2 · Stuffed tofu — niang doufu二 · 酿豆腐

The Hakka have a peasant inheritance and a wheat memory: their ancestors, from north of the Yellow River, ate dumplings. When they migrated south, into rice country, wheat became expensive and rare. The folk story goes that Hakka women, missing dumplings, began stuffing pork mince into the next-closest thing — soft tofu. The result, niang doufu (酿豆腐), is a cube of fresh tofu hollowed and filled with a small ball of seasoned pork, then pan-fried golden, then braised briefly in a clay pot with stock and scallion. It is a perfect dish: simple, economical, and visibly a wheat-noodle technique applied to a soybean substrate.客家有农耕传统、也有麦食记忆——祖上来自黄河以北，吃饺子。南迁之后到了稻区，麦贵稀少。民间传说是，客家妇女想念饺子，就把猪肉馅塞进最像饺子皮的东西——嫩豆腐。结果便是酿豆腐：一块鲜豆腐挖出小坑，填进调味的猪肉小球，煎到金黄，再下砂锅以高汤稍焖，撒葱花。这是一道近乎完美的菜：简、省，并且看得见——是用饺子的技法套到一个豆制品上。

3 · The clay pot — bao三 · 砂锅 — 煲

If the brine pot is Chaoshan's signature vessel, the clay pot is the Hakka's. Three-cup chicken (三杯鸡), claypot rice (煲仔饭), three-yellow chicken with bamboo (竹筒鸡) — these are stew-and-rest dishes where the unglazed clay holds heat evenly and slowly, transforming a tough cut into a forgiving one. A serious Hakka kitchen "seasons" its pots over years; oil and steam stain the inside dark, and a well-loved bao is said to flavor its own contents.如果说卤水锅是潮汕的标志器皿，砂锅就是客家的。三杯鸡、煲仔饭、竹筒鸡——这些都是"焖一焖、闷一闷"的菜。无釉砂锅让热量传得平、传得慢，把粗韧的部位炖软。认真的客家厨房会"养锅"——油与水汽常年熏在内壁上，老砂锅，据说能自行给食物加味。

4 · The Hakka diaspora四 · 客家在海外

The Hakka are everywhere. Lee Kuan Yew was Hakka. Sun Yat-sen was Hakka. Hakka kitchens turn up in Kolkata's Tangra district, in Mauritius, in Suriname, in Sabah. The diaspora kept the broad strokes — salt, the clay pot, stuffed tofu — and added what was at hand. In Kolkata, Hakka-Chinese cooks invented chili chicken with green capsicum and dark soy. In Mauritius, ginger and turmeric crept into stews. The Hakka kitchen, designed for the road, did what it was designed to do.客家人无处不在。李光耀是客家。孙中山是客家。从加尔各答的塘加拉区到毛里求斯，到苏里南，到沙巴，都有客家馆子。海外的客家菜保住了大脉——咸味、砂锅、酿豆腐——并按手边食材添东西。加尔各答的客籍华人发明了配青椒老抽的辣子鸡；毛里求斯的炖菜里渗进了姜与姜黄。这间为长路而设计的厨房，做完了它本来就该做的事。

Dishes to know这几道，得知道

Salt-baked chicken盐焗鸡

yán jú jī

Whole chicken roasted under hot coarse salt — the museum piece of Hakka technique.整鸡埋粗盐焗熟。客家技法的"博物馆藏"。

Stuffed tofu酿豆腐

niàng dòufu

Tofu cubes filled with pork — a wheat-dumpling technique transplanted onto soybean.豆腐里塞肉馅——把饺子的技术搬到豆腐上。

Mui choy kau yuk梅菜扣肉

méi cài kòu ròu

Pork belly braised over preserved mustard greens, inverted on the plate.五花肉与梅干菜同蒸，倒扣装盘。

Three-cup chicken三杯鸡

sān bēi jī

A cup of soy, a cup of rice wine, a cup of sesame oil, basil. A clay pot. A simple miracle.一杯酱油，一杯米酒，一杯麻油，加九层塔。砂锅一只。一个简单的奇迹。

Lei cha (pounded tea rice)擂茶

léi chá

A bowl of green tea, sesame, peanuts, and herbs pounded in a clay mortar, eaten over rice.陶钵里舂的绿茶、芝麻、花生与药草，浇在饭上吃。

Hakka stew (yong tau fu)客家煲

kè jiā bāo

Pork, dried tofu, stuffed bitter melon, vermicelli — all in a single clay pot.猪肉、豆腐干、酿苦瓜、粉丝，一只砂锅一锅熟。
