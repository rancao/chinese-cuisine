Cantonese · 粤菜

The cuisine of restraint. Where a Sichuan kitchen adds, a Cantonese kitchen subtracts. Steamed fish needs ginger and scallion and nothing else. The point is to disappear into the ingredient.克制的菜系。川菜做加法，粤菜做减法。一条清蒸鱼只要姜与葱，再不加东西。重点是让人尝到那条鱼，而不是那位厨子。

Cantonese cooking — Yue cai (粤菜) — is, by both reputation and self-conception, the most ingredient-honest of the major Chinese cuisines. The ideal of a Cantonese chef is to bring an ingredient to the table as itself, perfectly itself, and nothing else. This is harder than it sounds. A bad Sichuan dish can be saved with another shake of chili. A bad Cantonese steamed fish cannot be saved at all.粤菜——无论在外界评价还是它的自我认知里——都是中国八大菜系中最讲"食材诚实"的那一支。粤厨的理想，是把一种食材以它本来的样子端上桌，恰恰好的它自己，再无其他。这听上去简单，做起来比写起来难。一道川菜做坏了，再撒一勺辣椒就救得回。一条蒸鱼做坏了，谁也救不回。

1 · Dim sum — yum cha一 · 点心 · 饮茶

The yum cha tradition — "drinking tea," a euphemism for the entire weekend ritual of small plates and family — is the most international export of Cantonese cooking. A serious dim sum restaurant offers, at any hour, fifty to a hundred small dishes: shrimp dumplings (har gow), pork-and-shrimp dumplings (siu mai), barbecue pork buns (cha siu bao), rice rolls (cheung fun), egg tarts, lotus-paste sesame balls, beef tripe with white radish, tofu skin rolls in oyster sauce. The kitchen prepares each on a different rhythm; the dining room cycles them out on bamboo steamers stacked seven high. A two-hour dim sum lunch in Hong Kong is a marathon of micro-meals."饮茶"——口头讲是"喝茶"，实际是周末一家人围一桌、点心车一辆辆推过来的整套仪式——是粤菜最具国际辐射力的输出。一家像样的点心铺，任何时段都有五十到一百样小点：虾饺、烧麦、叉烧包、肠粉、蛋挞、麻团、白萝卜炖牛百叶、蚝油腐皮卷。厨房每样按自己的节奏出，餐厅每隔几分钟来一辆叠了七层的小笼车。在香港吃两个小时的早茶，是一连串小餐的马拉松。

2 · The double-boiled soup — lou fo tong二 · 老火汤 · 隔水炖

If dim sum is the public-facing Cantonese face, lou fo tong (老火汤, "old-fire soup") is the private one. A Cantonese household considers a meal incomplete without a thin, clear, deeply medicinal broth simmered for three to six hours from a few unassuming raw materials: lean pork, a smoked chicken bone, a slice of dried tangerine peel, a handful of fox-nut seeds, perhaps a hidden piece of cured ham. The technique is double-boiling (隔水炖) — the ingredients sit in a sealed ceramic jar that itself sits in a larger pot of simmering water, so the soup never approaches a hard boil, and the broth stays absolutely clear. A Cantonese aunt will tell you, with full conviction, which soup to drink in the third week of August and why.如果说点心是粤菜的公众面孔，老火汤就是它在家的样子。广东人家觉得，一顿饭里没有一碗清、薄、带药感的汤——文火炖三到六小时，几味平常食材：瘦肉、烟熏鸡骨、一片陈皮、一把芡实、藏一块咸火腿——这顿饭就不完整。技法是"隔水炖"：食材进瓷盅，瓷盅再放进一口大锅微沸水里。汤不滚，因此汤色一直清透。粤地的阿姨会很认真地告诉你，八月第三周该喝什么汤，为什么。

3 · The seafood cult — Hong Kong's wet markets三 · 鲜的信仰 · 香港湿货市场

On any given morning in Sai Ying Pun or Mong Kok, a Cantonese fish stall holds the day's catch in fifty separate tanks of running seawater. The seller knows the boats by name. A customer points to a live grouper; the stall hand nets it, weighs it, hits it once on the head, scales it, and you walk it home in a plastic bag. By 7 p.m. that fish will have been steamed for nine minutes — never ten — with ginger, scallion, a few drops of hot peanut oil, and a precise tablespoon of light soy. Anything more is, in Cantonese terms, an insult to the fish.香港西营盘或旺角的任何一个早上，一档粤式鱼摊把当天的渔获分装在五十多缸活水里。摊主认得船的名字。客人指一条石斑；摊伙网起来、过秤、一记敲晕、刮鳞，塑料袋拎回家。当晚七点，这条鱼会被清蒸九分钟——绝不到十——加姜、葱、几滴热花生油、一勺生抽。再多加东西，按粤人的话讲，就是对这条鱼不礼貌。

4 · Roast meats — siu mei四 · 烧腊 · 烧味

Cantonese siu mei (烧味) — char siu, roast pork belly, roast goose, soy-marinated chicken — is the everyday meat counter of every Cantonese neighborhood. The technique is hot-oven roasting on hooks, often in dedicated wood-fired or gas-fired vertical ovens; the marinades involve maltose, five-spice, fermented red bean curd, and shaoxing wine; the cuts are precise (a single chop, never sawed). A bowl of rice, two slices of char siu, a spoon of pickled vegetable: this is lunch for half of Hong Kong.粤式烧腊——叉烧、烧肉、烧鹅、豉油鸡——是粤地每个街区肉档的日常底。技法是挂炉烤，常用木火或燃气直立炉；腌料里有麦芽糖、五香、南乳、绍兴酒；下刀讲究（一刀斩落，绝不锯）。一碗白饭、两片叉烧、一勺腌菜，是香港一半人的午餐。

5 · Beyond Guangzhou — the wider Yue五 · 不止广州 · 更大的粤

Yue cai is conventionally divided into three sub-traditions: Guangzhou (the canonical center), Shunde (the technical school: many of China's best chefs come from this single delta city), and Hakka-Cantonese fringe cooking (treated separately, see Hakka). Shunde is worth a footnote: the city of Shunde, 50 kilometers downstream from Guangzhou, has the highest density of culinary masters per capita in China. The "fish-in-eight-cuts" knife work that defines a Cantonese banquet is a Shunde invention.粤菜传统上分三支：广州（教科书中心）、顺德（技术派——中国最好的厨子有相当一部分出自这座三角洲小城）、客家广府边缘（另列，见客家篇）。顺德值得单提：这座广州下游 50 公里的城，是中国人均烹饪大师密度最高的地方。粤式宴席里那套"鱼分八式"的刀工，是顺德发明的。

Dishes to know这几道，得知道

Steamed sea bass清蒸石斑

qīng zhēng shí bān

Whole fish, ginger, scallion, hot oil, soy. Nine minutes. The test of every Cantonese kitchen.整鱼、姜、葱、热油、生抽。九分钟。每家粤馆的试金石。

Char siu叉烧

chā shāo

Honey-glazed barbecue pork. A good one has a slight char at the edges and a tender pink interior.蜜汁烤猪。好叉烧边缘略焦，中心粉嫩。

Har gow (shrimp dumplings)虾饺

xiā jiǎo

A translucent wheat-starch skin pleated 13 times around a whole prawn. The benchmark dim sum.澄面皮褶 13 折包整粒鲜虾。点心里的标尺。

Cheung fun肠粉

cháng fěn

A sheet of silky rice noodle, rolled around shrimp or barbecue pork, dressed with sweet soy.薄如绸的米浆皮裹虾或叉烧，淋甜豉油。

Roast goose烧鹅

shāo é

Hong-Kong style. Five-spice marinade, lacquered skin, fat that lifts off the bone.港式。五香腌、皮亮如漆、脂肉离骨。

Old-fire soup老火汤

lǎo huǒ tāng

A medicinal soup, simmered for hours, drunk before the meal begins.滋补的清汤，文火炖数小时，饭前先喝。
