Hainan · 海南

A small island with disproportionate culinary weight. China's only fully tropical province cooks lighter, sourer, more coconut-flecked than anywhere else on the mainland.一座小岛，分量却不小。中国唯一彻底的热带省份，做菜比大陆任何地方都更清、更酸、更带椰子味。

Hainan was, for most of imperial Chinese history, a place of exile. Su Shi was banished to Danzhou in 1097 and famously wrote that the only thing the island lacked was good wine. Today the island is a vacation province with five-star resorts on the southern beaches, but the cooking — particularly inland and on the eastern coast — has held its shape. It is a cuisine of poached chicken, slow-cooked goat, coconut-water rice, and an almost Cantonese reverence for the unaltered ingredient.海南，整个帝制时代多半时间是流放之地。苏东坡 1097 年被贬儋州，名言"此间但无好酒"。今天它是个度假省，南边沙滩上立着五星酒店，但岛的内陆与东海岸——做菜的轮廓没变。这是一门讲究白切鸡、慢炖羊、椰水煮饭、对原食材有近乎粤菜般敬意的菜。

1 · The four famous dishes一 · 四大名菜

Hainan has codified its top four dishes — 海南四大名菜 — into a set: Wenchang chicken, Jiaji duck, Hele crab, and Dongshan goat. The grammar is identical: each is named for the village or county where the breed is raised, and each is cooked with deliberate restraint. The point is to taste the animal, not the technique.海南把它最具代表性的四道菜钦定为"四大名菜"——文昌鸡、加积鸭、和乐蟹、东山羊。语法是一样的：每一道以养殖地命名，做法都是有意克制——重点是吃出那只动物，而不是技术本身。

Wenchang chicken is the most famous of the four. The breed is a slim, long-legged chicken raised in Wenchang county, finished on a diet of fallen banyan figs that gives the fat a faint sweet edge. The cooking is the Cantonese poach-and-rest: a whole bird is dropped into stock just under a boil, taken off the heat, and left to finish in residual warmth. The result is a chicken with a translucent yellow skin, a layer of soft fat, and white, supple meat. It is sliced bone-in and served with three small dishes of dip: ginger-scallion oil, sweet soy, and the famously orange chicken-fat-and-tangerine sauce. A bowl of chicken-broth rice (鸡饭) is served on the side. Singapore's "Hainanese chicken rice" comes from this dish, brought by Hainanese cooks who emigrated in the 1920s.文昌鸡是四大里最出名的。品种是文昌县养的瘦腿长身土鸡，最后一段以落榕果增肥，让脂肪带一丝甜。做法是粤式的"白切"——整鸡入将沸未沸的汤、离火、用余温焗熟。结果是一只皮薄而黄透、皮下一层软脂、肉细的鸡。带骨斩件，配三碟蘸料：姜葱油、甜豉油、以及那种橙黄色的鸡油橘子皮酱。一碗鸡油饭并上。新加坡的"海南鸡饭"，就是从这道菜来的——1920 年代海南厨工出洋带去的。

Dongshan goat (东山羊) is the more powerful counterpart. A small black-coated goat from Dongshan town, fed on a hillside of fragrant grasses, slow-braised with white radish and a clay-pot's worth of rice wine. The meat has none of the gaminess of a winter mutton; it tastes like the hill it grew on.东山羊是更厚重的那一支——东山镇出产的小黑山羊，吃岭上香草长大，与白萝卜、一砂锅米酒同炖。肉没有冬羊的膻气，吃出的是它长大的那座山。

2 · Rice noodles by ten thousand names二 · 米粉的一万个名字

Almost every Hainan town has its own breakfast rice-noodle dish: Haikou's Hainan fen (海南粉) with peanuts, fried tofu, and shredded daikon; Lingao's bowl-noodle (临高米粉) in a clear fish soup; Houan's beef-rice-noodle (后安粉) with a black-bean-paste gravy; the Wenchang abalone noodle on festival mornings. The unifying texture is a slightly broader, slightly softer rice noodle than the mainland would produce — the island has its own short-grain rice, and the noodles taste of it.几乎每个海南镇都有自己的早餐米粉：海口的海南粉（拌花生、油豆腐、萝卜干丝）；临高的清鱼汤米粉；后安的豉油牛肉粉；文昌节日早上的鲍鱼粉。统一的口感是比大陆略宽、略软的米粉——岛上有它自己的短粒稻，做出的粉味道也跟着不一样。

3 · Coconut, the secret ingredient三 · 椰子，藏起来的食材

Hainan is the only Chinese province where coconut palms grow as a serious crop. The result, almost everywhere, is a quiet sweetness in soups and rices that gives Hainanese food a Southeast Asian feel. Coconut-water chicken soup (椰子鸡), the dish of the moment in mainland-tier-one cities, has been simmering in Wenchang kitchens for two centuries: a young coconut is sawed open, the water poured into a pot with the bird, and the cooled flesh is added back at the end. There is also a coconut-rice cake (椰糕) served at weddings, and a coconut-flesh-and-pork stew (椰丝焖五花) that tastes like a Thai aunt's secret.海南是中国唯一把椰子树当正经作物种的省。结果是几乎处处都有一点不张扬的甜——把海南菜往东南亚那一边推了半步。椰子鸡，最近几年北上一线城市流行的那道，在文昌厨房里炖了两百年：青椰锯开，椰水倒进锅、整鸡同煮，椰肉最后回锅。还有婚宴上的椰糕、椰丝焖五花——尝起来像泰国阿姨的家传。

4 · The Li and Miao kitchens四 · 黎族与苗族的厨房

In the interior mountains of central Hainan, the Li (黎) and Miao (苗) peoples cook a very different cuisine: sour-soup fish (鱼酸汤) similar to the Dong-Miao food of Guizhou, sticky rice cooked in green bamboo (竹筒饭), and a much-celebrated "five-color rice" stained with edible plant pigments for festivals. Their food is rarely served in tourist Haikou, which is its own loss. Find it on a Wuzhishan hill road and the trip pays for itself.海南中部山区，黎族与苗族做着完全不同的菜：鱼酸汤——和贵州侗苗一系相近；竹筒饭——糯米塞青竹筒在火上烧熟；以及节日上那道"五色饭"，用可食用草本染色。这些菜很少出现在游客版海口，遗憾就在这里。要在五指山一条公路边的小馆子里找——找到就值回票价。

Dishes to know这几道，得知道

Wenchang chicken文昌鸡

wén chāng jī

A poached island chicken with three dips — the root of Singaporean chicken rice.白切的岛鸡配三碟蘸料——新加坡海南鸡饭的源头。

Dongshan goat东山羊

dōng shān yáng

A small black mountain goat, slow-braised with daikon and rice wine.东山小黑羊，与白萝卜、米酒同炖。

Coconut chicken pot椰子鸡

yēzi jī

A young coconut, an island chicken, two hours of patience.青椰、岛鸡、两小时耐心。

Hainan rice noodles海南粉

hǎi nán fěn

A "dry" noodle: rice noodles tossed with peanuts, fried tofu, and a hint of fish sauce."干捞"式：米粉拌花生、油豆腐、一点鱼露。

Hele crab和乐蟹

hé lè xiè

A small Hele mudcrab, steamed in shochu, eaten with vinegar-ginger.和乐小膏蟹，米酒清蒸，蘸醋姜。

Sour soup fish (Li)黎家酸汤鱼

suān tāng yú

A tropical sour-fish soup with wild tomato and Vietnamese coriander.野番茄、越南香菜做底，热带版的酸汤鱼。
