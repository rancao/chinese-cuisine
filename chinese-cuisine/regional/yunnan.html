Yunnan · 云南

Twenty-five ethnic groups, ten thousand foot of altitude, four hundred edible mushrooms in a single rainy season. Yunnan is not a province; it is a continent of microclimates that happens to have one government.二十五个民族，海拔三千余米，一个雨季四百多种可食用菌。云南不是一个省，它是恰好共用同一个省政府的一片大陆，里头藏着无数微气候。

If you ask a Chinese chef which province has the strangest pantry, they almost always say Yunnan. A subtropical south slides upward into temperate plateaus and then again into Tibetan-edge alpine country, all in a single drive. Each elevation grows different things. Each climate ferments differently. Each minority — Dai, Bai, Hani, Naxi, Wa, Yi — cooks differently. The result is the most ingredient-rich cuisine in China, and the hardest to summarize.问一个中国厨子，哪个省的食材最奇怪，答案多半是云南。亚热带的南边，缓缓抬升成温带高原，再上去就是藏区边缘的高山带，整个过程在一次自驾里就走完。每个海拔生长的东西不同。每种气候发酵的方式不同。每个少数民族——傣、白、哈尼、纳西、佤、彝——做法都不一样。结果就是：中国食材最丰富、最难概括的菜系。

We resist the urge to flatten. What follows is a brief geography: the four mini-traditions that make up the Yunnan plate, and the ingredients that make each one possible.我们试着不把它压扁。下面是一份简略地理：构成"云南菜"那一盘的四个小传统，以及让每个传统成立的食材。

1 · Mushrooms — the June miracle一 · 菌子 — 六月的奇迹

Every June, when the rains arrive in the Yunnan highlands, the forests fruit. Locals call it the "mushroom season" (菌子季节) and the entire province reorganizes around it. Markets that sold mostly vegetables in May open dedicated mushroom wings. Restaurants reprint menus weekly. The most prized — porcini (牛肝菌), matsutake (松茸), termite mushroom (鸡枞), morels (羊肚菌), and the cautious green-head (青头菌) — each have a window of two to six weeks. Miss it, and you wait a year.每年六月雨水落到云南高地，森林开始出菇。本地人叫"菌子季节"，整个省围着它重组。五月还主营蔬菜的菜市场会单辟"菌子区"。餐厅每周改菜单。最金贵的——牛肝菌、松茸、鸡枞、羊肚菌、谨慎食用的青头菌——每种只有两到六周的窗口期。错过，再等一年。

A Yunnan mushroom-fueled meal is a particular thing: a clear chicken stock with cubes of porcini, an oyster-mushroom and Sichuan-pepper stir-fry, a small earthen pot of stewed wild fungi that the host instructs you not to touch for ten minutes — yes, even that one — because under-stewed Boletus can play interesting tricks on the nervous system. Yunnan eaters say this with affection, not fear.一顿以菌子为骨的云南饭，是这样的：清炖鸡汤里浮着牛肝菌块；平菇配花椒爆炒；一只小砂锅里慢炖野生菌，主人会让你十分钟内不许动筷——是的，包括看起来已经熟透那一锅——因为牛肝菌没炖透，会跟神经系统开点玩笑。云南人说这话时是带笑的，不是怕。

2 · Crossing-the-Bridge Noodles二 · 过桥米线

If Yunnan has one dish the rest of China knows, it is mixian — rice noodles — and specifically Crossing-the-Bridge Noodles (过桥米线). The legend involves a Qing-dynasty scholar studying on an island, his wife carrying lunch across a bridge, and the discovery that a thick layer of chicken fat could keep a broth hot enough to cook delicate slices of meat on contact. Whether or not the story is true, the construction is real: a bowl of very hot, very fatty broth arrives at the table, followed by a regiment of small plates — raw pork, chicken, quail egg, scallion, chrysanthemum petals, soaked rice noodles — to be added in a specific order. The hottest goes first; the noodles last.如果说云南有一道菜全国都知道，那是米线，更具体地说是过桥米线。传说讲的是清代一位书生在岛上读书，妻子送饭要过一座桥，意外发现一层厚厚的鸡油能让汤热到把生薄片烫熟。传说真假难辨，但结构是真的：一只滚烫油亮的汤碗先到桌上，紧跟着是一队小碟——生猪肉、鸡肉、鹌鹑蛋、葱花、菊花瓣、泡软的米线——按特定顺序投入。最难熟的先下，米线最后。

There are perhaps thirty serious Crossing-the-Bridge shops in Kunming, and they have philosophical differences about the broth. Some are old-hen-and-pork-bone for hours. Some add Yunnan ham. Almost none are good outside the province; the noodles do not travel.昆明做过桥米线的认真馆子约莫三十家，关于汤的派别都不一样。有的老母鸡加猪骨熬几小时。有的加云南火腿。几乎没有一家在云南省外是好吃的——米线不远行。

3 · Dai food — the southern hot edge三 · 傣味 — 南边的辣酸

In Xishuangbanna and along the Mekong, Yunnan becomes Southeast Asia. The Dai people share roots with the Tai of Thailand and the Shan of Myanmar, and their food shows it: pounded green-papaya salad, charred-eggplant dip with cilantro, lemongrass-stuffed grilled fish, sour-spicy beef tartare, sticky rice eaten with the fingers from a small bamboo basket. The seasoning is herbal and bright — kaffir lime leaf, sawtooth coriander, wild chili — and the technique is grill-and-pound, not stir-fry.在西双版纳与澜沧江沿岸，云南变成了东南亚。傣族与泰国的泰人、缅甸的掸人同源，餐桌上看得见这层血脉：木臼舂的青木瓜沙拉、香菜烤茄子蘸水、香茅塞肚子的烤鱼、酸辣牛肉刺身、用手从竹篓里捻出的糯米饭。调味是草本系的、亮的——青柠叶、刺芫荽、野山椒——技法是烤与舂，而不是炒。

A Dai meal looks nothing like a Sichuan meal. It is greener, more sour, more aggressively herbal. The chili is fresh-cut, not pre-fried in oil. The starch is glutinous and eaten with the hand. If a Cantonese kitchen aims at a kind of clarity, the Dai kitchen aims at brightness — a tangle of leaves you can name only half of.一顿傣味与一顿川菜全然不同。它更绿、更酸、更草本到刺鼻。辣椒是鲜切，不在油里先煸过。主食是糯米，徒手抓着吃。如果说粤菜追求清，傣菜追求亮——一把你只能叫出一半名字的草叶。

4 · Ham country — Xuanwei & Nuodeng四 · 火腿之乡 — 宣威与诺邓

Two Yunnan towns produce ham of the kind that gets reverently shaved at the Spanish-jamón end of the cured-meat world. Xuanwei ham, salted in the cold months and air-cured for at least a year, is the workhorse: a deep red, salty edge to soups, stuffings, and the famous "ham mooncake" of mid-autumn. Nuodeng ham, made by the Bai people of a salt-mining village, is the rarer specimen — cured with brine drawn from a 3,000-year-old well, hung for three years, sliced thin and eaten raw. Both depend on altitude, low humidity, and the kind of long winter that lets time finish the work.云南有两个出火腿的地方，水准放在西班牙伊比利亚那一档也站得住。宣威火腿是主力——寒月入盐，风干至少一年，深红咸口，能为汤、为馅、为中秋的"云腿月饼"撑骨。诺邓火腿更稀——白族在一个盐井村庄里做，用一口三千年的老盐井卤水腌制，挂三年，薄切生食。两者都靠海拔、低湿，以及那种"让时间替你把活干完"的漫长冬天。

A note on the ham mooncake (云腿月饼). The Cantonese will tell you a real mooncake is lotus seed paste with a salted yolk. The Yunnan position, politely but firmly, is that a real mooncake is a flaky, lard-shortened pastry shell wrapped around chopped Xuanwei ham and honey. Both are real. Both are correct. Eat both.一句关于云腿月饼。广东人会告诉你，真月饼是莲蓉咸黄。云南立场，礼貌但坚定：真月饼是猪油起酥的饼皮里包着切丁宣腿和蜂蜜。两种都是真的。两种都对。两种都吃。

Dishes to know这几道，得知道

Crossing-the-Bridge Noodles过桥米线

guò qiáo mǐxiàn

A fat-sealed broth that cooks raw ingredients on contact. The defining dish of Kunming.一层油封住汤温，生料下去即熟。昆明的招牌。

Steam-pot chicken汽锅鸡

qì guō jī

A purpose-shaped clay pot collects only condensed steam — the clearest, most concentrated chicken broth in China.一种特制陶锅只接蒸汽冷凝水，做出全中国最清、最浓的鸡汤。

Wild mushroom hot pot野生菌火锅

yěshēng jūn huǒguō

A clear broth, fifteen kinds of fungi, a ten-minute rule before the first bite.清汤底，十五种菌，"煮够十分钟才许动筷"的规矩。

Dai pineapple rice傣味菠萝饭

bōluó fàn

Glutinous rice and dried pork floss steamed inside a hollowed pineapple. A southern-Yunnan staple.糯米与肉松在掏空的菠萝里蒸熟。云南南部常见。

Erkuai饵块

ěr kuài

A dense rice cake — grilled, brushed with chili-bean sauce, folded around a youtiao. Kunming's breakfast.压紧的米饼——炭烤、刷豆瓣酱、卷一根油条。昆明的早餐。

Charred-eggplant dip傣味茄子蘸水

qiézi zhànshuǐ

Eggplant burned in the embers, peeled, pounded with chili, garlic, cilantro. Served as a dip.茄子在炭火里烧透，剥皮，与辣椒、蒜、芫荽一起舂成蘸水。

What to drink with it配什么喝

Pu'er tea, of course — Yunnan's other great export, fermented and pressed into bricks that age like wine. But also: rice wine flavored with bayberry, and in the Dai south, a lemongrass-infused rice liquor that is closer to a Thai apertif than a Chinese baijiu.普洱茶，当然——云南另一项大宗，发酵压饼，越陈越贵。还有：杨梅泡的米酒；在傣区南部，香茅泡的米酒，喝起来更像泰国的开胃酒，而不像中国的白酒。
