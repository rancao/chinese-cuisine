Twelve misconceptions, addressed十二条常见误解

Common Western beliefs about Chinese food, taken seriously, addressed patiently, and — where they are wrong — gently corrected.西方对中餐的常见说法，我们认真对待，耐心回应，错的地方——温和地纠正。

We have collected the twelve assumptions about Chinese food we hear most often from Western friends, journalists, and travelers. Some are myths; some have a small truth at the center of a large misunderstanding; some are entirely correct and we will admit so. This essay tries to give each its fair hearing.我们收集了西方朋友、记者、游客身上最常听到的十二条关于中餐的"印象"。有的是误解；有的是大误解里包着一粒小事实；有的就是真的，我们也承认。这一篇试着对每一条公道地说说话。

1. "All Chinese food is greasy.""中餐都很油。"

No. Cantonese steamed fish has no added oil. A Hangzhou tea-fragrant chicken uses a teaspoon. Hot pot is broth-based. The "greasy" perception comes from the American Chinese takeout subset — heavy fried-and-glazed dishes that are American-Chinese, not Chinese. The actual home-style Chinese kitchen runs leaner than the Italian one.不对。粤式清蒸鱼不加油。杭州龙井虾仁用一茶匙。火锅是清汤。"油"的印象多来自美式中餐外卖那一小块——重炸重酱的菜。本土中餐家常做法比意餐还瘦。

2. "MSG is dangerous.""味精有害。"

No. Monosodium glutamate is the sodium salt of glutamate, an amino acid that occurs naturally in tomato, Parmesan, mushroom, and human breast milk. The "Chinese restaurant syndrome" panic of the 1960s was traced to a single 1968 letter to the New England Journal of Medicine and has not been replicated in double-blind studies. The FDA, WHO, and EU food-safety authorities all consider MSG safe at culinary levels. Salt — sodium chloride — is genuinely worse for you. We feel obliged to put this on the record.不对。味精是谷氨酸的钠盐——谷氨酸天然存在于番茄、帕玛森奶酪、蘑菇和人母乳里。1960 年代的"中餐馆综合症"恐慌可追溯到 1968 年《新英格兰医学杂志》的一封读者来信，从未在双盲试验里被复现。FDA、WHO 与欧盟食品安全机构都认定味精在烹饪剂量下安全。盐——氯化钠——对身体的影响更糟。这件事我们觉得有必要写下来。

3. "Fortune cookies are Chinese.""签语饼是中国的。"

No. They are Japanese in origin — a 19th-century rice cracker from a Kyoto temple area — and were introduced to America via the Japanese community of late-Meiji San Francisco. They migrated into Chinese-American restaurants during WWII, when Japanese-American chefs were interned and Chinese-American bakeries took over their machines. See our dedicated essay.不是。它源自日本——19 世纪京都神社一带的米饼——经晚明治旧金山的日裔社区进入美国。二战日裔被拘禁后，他们的烤模被华裔接手，签语饼就此进入美式中餐。详见专篇。

4. "Chinese people eat dog.""中国人吃狗。"

Vanishingly rarely. Dog meat is eaten in two very specific, very small parts of China (parts of Guangxi, a few Korean-influenced Yanbian villages) and the practice is widely seen as either embarrassing or actively distasteful by most Chinese people. The annual Yulin dog-meat festival, which drew international attention in the 2010s, was a local event opposed by most of the national public. Comparing this to "Chinese people eat dog" is like comparing "some Texans eat rattlesnake" to "Americans eat snake."极罕见。狗肉在中国只有两小块地方吃——广西部分地区、延边几个朝鲜族影响的村——而且大多数中国人对这件事的态度是尴尬或反感。2010 年代被国际关注的玉林狗肉节是地方事件，国内主流舆论是反对的。把它泛化为"中国人吃狗"，相当于把"得克萨斯几个人吃响尾蛇"说成"美国人吃蛇"。

5. "All Chinese food is spicy.""中餐都辣。"

No — only four of China's eight major cuisines use chili meaningfully. Cantonese cooking is famously mild. Shandong, Huaiyang, Fujian, and Anhui kitchens use almost no chili. Even Sichuan, the famous heat capital, codifies twenty-three flavor profiles of which only seven are spicy. The "spicy" stereotype is mostly downstream of a few headline dishes that traveled well (mapo tofu, kung pao) and overrepresented the heat-using regions.不对——中国八大菜系里只有四个真正用辣椒。粤菜以清淡出名。鲁、淮扬、闽、徽几乎不用辣。即便川菜——出名的辣都——也有二十三种味型，其中只有七种是辣的。"中餐都辣"的印象，多半来自几道传得最广的菜（麻婆、宫保）让吃辣的省份"超额代表"了。

6. "Chinese people don't eat dessert.""中国人不吃甜点。"

Partially true. China has a less developed dessert course than France or Italy — sweet items rarely conclude a meal — but Chinese desserts exist and are wonderful: tang yuan (sweet glutinous rice balls in soup), red-bean soup, mooncakes, almond tofu, sweet sesame paste, snowy mochi from Suzhou, Cantonese double-boiled sweet soups. These are usually eaten as a separate occasion rather than after dinner. The structural difference is real; the absence is overstated.部分对。中餐的甜品环节不像法、意那么发达——很少作为正餐的收尾——但甜品确实存在，且做得很好：汤圆、红豆汤、月饼、杏仁豆腐、芝麻糊、苏州雪糕、粤式炖糖水。它们多半作为单独的"吃甜"场合存在，不在饭后。结构性差异是真的；"没有"则被夸大了。

7. "Chinese cooking uses no cheese.""中餐不用奶酪。"

Mostly true. China's dairy tradition is geographically marginal — mainly the Mongolian, Tibetan, and Yunnanese highland kitchens. Yunnan has a fried-cheese pancake (乳扇) and a sliced cheese (乳饼) that are genuinely cheese-like. But Han Chinese kitchens are essentially dairy-free, a fact rooted in lactose intolerance rates (~85% in Han Chinese adults) and an agricultural pattern that did not center on dairy animals.基本对。中国的乳制品传统集中在边缘地区——蒙古、藏区、滇高原。云南有油煎的乳扇与切片的乳饼，是真正意义上的"奶酪"。但汉族厨房基本无乳——根源在乳糖不耐（汉族成人约 85%）与历来不以乳畜为中心的农业模式。

8. "Real Chinese food is whatever I had in Beijing/Shanghai/Hong Kong.""我在北京/上海/香港吃过的，才是真中餐。"

A common mistake. Whichever Chinese city you visited has only one of eight major cuisines plus a city-specific street culture. A two-week trip to Shanghai teaches you Shanghainese and a small slice of Jiangsu — it does not teach you Sichuan, Hunan, Yunnan, or Xinjiang. The country is too big for any one stop to be representative.常见误解。你去过的那座城，只有八大菜系之一，加上城市自身的街吃。两周的上海行教你上海菜与少许江苏菜——它不教你川、湘、滇、新。这个国家太大，任何一站都不是代表样本。

9. "Chinese people drink only tea, not water.""中国人不喝水，只喝茶。"

No, though there is a related truth: warm or hot water is widely preferred to cold water, including with meals. This is a 20th-century public-health legacy (encouraging boiled water to prevent waterborne disease) that has become a cultural habit. Tea is drunk widely but not exclusively. A Chinese restaurant's first move is to ask whether you want hot tea or hot water, never cold anything. This regularly disorients Western visitors.不是——不过有一条相关的真理：中国普遍偏好喝温水或热水，吃饭时也是。这是 20 世纪公共卫生的遗产（鼓励喝开水以防水源病），后来变成文化习惯。茶喝得很普遍，但不是唯一。中餐馆上桌第一件事是问你要热茶还是热水，从不问冷的。这经常让西方客人困惑。

10. "Chopsticks have always been used for everything.""中国人一直都用筷子吃所有东西。"

Not quite. Chopsticks were originally cooking tools (for retrieving items from hot oil and broth); their adoption as eating utensils spread in the Han dynasty (2nd century BCE onward). Soup spoons have always been used alongside. Rice was eaten with chopsticks in the south but often with hands or wooden paddles in the north until well into the Ming. The "chopsticks for everything" picture is broadly true today but reflects a long evolution.不完全对。筷子最初是炊具（从热油热汤里捞东西），作为餐具普及是从汉代（前 2 世纪后）开始的。汤匙一直与之并用。米饭在南方一直用筷子吃，但北方直到明代很多时候还用手或木瓢。"用筷子吃一切"在今天大致成立，但背后是漫长的演化。

11. "Chinese food doesn't have its own wine pairing tradition.""中餐没有酒水搭配传统。"

It does, but the grammar is different. Shaoxing rice wine has been paired with crab in Jiangnan for a thousand years. Baijiu — Chinese sorghum spirit — is the canonical match for a rich braise or a banquet. Phoenix Mountain oolong is the obligatory partner of a Chaoshan meal. Yunnan Pu'er is paired with rich pork or game. The pairings are codified within each region; what is missing is a unified national vocabulary like French wine pairing.有，只是语法不同。绍兴黄酒配江南大闸蟹有千年传统。白酒——中国高粱酒——是浓厚红烧和宴席的标准搭。凤凰单丛是潮汕餐桌的必备伴。云南普洱配厚重的肉与野味。每个地区都有自己的成体系搭配；缺的是法式餐酒那样的全国统一词汇表。

12. "Real Chinese restaurants don't have a menu in English.""真正的中餐馆没有英文菜单。"

A useful approximation, but not airtight. The most authentic kitchens in Western Chinatowns are often family operations where the English menu is short, simplified, or non-existent — and the Chinese-only menu has the real dishes. Equally, however, some of the best high-end Chinese restaurants in Western cities have full bilingual menus for tourist convenience. The English-menu test is a heuristic; do not make it a rule.这是个有用的近似，但不绝对。西方唐人街最地道的小馆子，英文菜单往往短、简化、甚至没有；中文菜单上才是真菜。但西方城市顶级中餐厅也常有完整双语菜单，方便游客。英文菜单测试是个经验法则，不是规则。
