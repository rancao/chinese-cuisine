American Chinese: an honest survey美式中餐：一份诚实的盘点

Chop suey, egg roll, sesame chicken, crab rangoon, beef and broccoli. Where each came from, why each tastes the way it does, and why pretending none of them exist is no help to anyone.杂碎、春卷、芝麻鸡、蟹角、芥兰牛。每一道从哪儿来，为什么是那个味道，以及——假装它们不存在帮不了谁。

American Chinese food is a cuisine. Not a corruption, not a knock-off, not "real Chinese with the heat turned down." It is its own kitchen, born of specific historical pressures, evolved over 150 years, with named dishes and named inventors and a clear regional center of gravity (New York for the East Coast version, San Francisco for the West Coast variant). It is also, by the volumes served, one of the most consumed cuisines on earth: in 2026 there are roughly 40,000 Chinese-American restaurants in the United States — more than McDonald's, Burger King, and Wendy's combined.美式中餐是一门菜系。不是变形、不是山寨、不是"把辣减了的真中餐"。它是自成一格的厨房，由具体的历史压力催生，演化了 150 年，有自己的名菜、有可考的发明者、有清晰的地理中心（东岸版的中心在纽约，西岸版在旧金山）。从消耗量看，它还是世界上最被吃得多的菜系之一：2026 年美国大约有 4 万家美式中餐馆——超过麦当劳、汉堡王、Wendy's 三家加起来。

This essay surveys the canon. Each dish gets a short biography. The aim is not authentication but understanding.这一篇是版本盘点。每道菜一份短传记。目的不是"正名"，是"理解"。

Chop suey (杂碎)杂碎

The original American-Chinese dish. The name is Cantonese — jaap seui, "miscellaneous bits" — and the dish entered the American imagination in the 1880s, served by Cantonese cooks to American mining and railway crews in the West. By 1903 it was on Manhattan restaurant menus. The recipe was elastic: any combination of stir-fried meat slivers, vegetables, and broth-thickened sauce, served over rice. Critics have called it a "garbage dish"; defenders have pointed out that it is, in fact, an instance of the older Cantonese homestyle category of "stir-fried bits" (杂炒), with a long Chinese pedigree. By the 1960s American taste had moved on. Chop suey is rarely seen now outside historical restaurants and small-town menus, where it lives as a nostalgia item.第一道美式中餐。名字是粤语"杂碎"——零碎七拼八凑——在 1880 年代由广东厨子做给西部矿工和铁路工人。1903 年上了曼哈顿的菜单。配方有弹性：任意几样肉丝、蔬菜、勾过芡的酱汁，盖饭上桌。批评家说它"垃圾菜"；辩护者指出，它其实是粤地家常"杂炒"的延伸，有正经的中餐谱系。1960 年代美国口味往别处去了。如今只在几家历史餐厅和小镇老菜单上留着，是一道"念旧菜"。

Egg roll & spring roll (蛋卷 / 春卷)蛋卷与春卷

In American Chinese, "egg roll" refers specifically to the thick, deep-fried, finger-thick cylinder wrapped in an egg-and-wheat skin, filled with cabbage and a small amount of pork. The "spring roll" of mainland China is the lighter cousin — a thin pastry skin, smaller, sometimes fried, sometimes fresh. The American egg roll is a 1930s-New-York innovation that thickened and supersized the original spring roll for the American taste. Both descend, distantly, from a Tang-dynasty spring-festival pancake.美式中餐里的 egg roll 专指那种粗、深炸、手指般厚的圆筒，皮是含蛋的厚面皮，馅是包心菜与少量猪肉。中国大陆的"春卷"是它的轻盈兄弟——薄皮、个头小、有炸有鲜。美式蛋卷是 1930 年代纽约的发明——把春卷加厚加大，迁就美国胃。两者都远远地继承自唐代立春的"春饼"。

Beef and broccoli (芥兰牛 → American)芥兰牛 → 美式版

A direct descendant of Cantonese gai lan ngau (Chinese broccoli with beef in oyster sauce), with one substitution: Chinese broccoli (gai lan) was unavailable in 1940s America, so cooks used Western broccoli florets instead. The substitute stuck. The American version is sweeter and more soy-heavy than the Cantonese original, which is sharper and more vegetal. A fair instance of a dish moving across an ocean and finding a new vegetable.直系来自粤式蚝油芥兰牛肉。只有一处替换：1940 年代美国买不到芥兰，厨子改用西兰花。替换沉淀了下来。美式版比粤版甜、酱重；粤版更锐利、更"青"。一道菜跨洋后找到了新蔬菜，公道地说。

Sesame chicken (芝麻鸡)芝麻鸡

A late-1970s sibling of General Tso's. Same deep-fried boneless chicken, similar sweet glaze, finished with toasted white sesame instead of dried chili. The dish is even further from any Chinese original than General Tso's; it is American to the bone. Three out of four Chinese-American restaurants in the United States carry it. Worth eating well-made.1970 年代末与左宗棠鸡同辈的兄弟。同样的去骨深炸鸡块，差不多的甜糖浆，最后撒烤白芝麻替代干辣椒。比左宗棠鸡离任何中国原版都更远，几乎彻头彻尾是美国菜。美国四家美式中餐馆里有三家在卖。做得好的，值得吃。

Crab rangoon (蟹角)蟹角

A wonton skin folded around a filling of cream cheese, crab (or imitation crab), garlic, and scallion, deep-fried until the skin blisters. Invented around 1956 at Trader Vic's, a tiki-themed restaurant chain — not by a Chinese-American kitchen, but by a Polynesian-themed American one borrowing wonton skins. Despite its origins, crab rangoon became part of the American Chinese canon. Almost no version exists outside North America. There is no equivalent dish in China.馄饨皮包奶油奶酪、蟹肉（或蟹棒）、蒜、葱，深炸到皮起泡。1956 年前后由 Trader Vic's——一家 tiki 主题美式连锁——发明，不是华裔厨房创造的，是一家波利尼西亚主题美式餐厅借了馄饨皮。尽管出身如此，蟹角成了美式中餐经典。北美以外几乎没有这道菜。中国本土也没有对应物。

Kung pao chicken (宫保鸡丁), American style美式宫保鸡丁

A real Sichuan dish (see our Sichuan page), but reshaped for the American palate: chili replaced with mild dried chili, peppercorn often omitted entirely, peanuts increased, and a brighter sweet-and-sour glaze added. The American kung pao is sometimes called "the dish that does not numb" by Sichuanese diners visiting the US; the absence of the peppercorn ma changes the dish entirely. Still: good American kung pao is good. We do not police.这是一道真川菜（见川菜页），但在美式版里被改造：辣椒换成温辣的干辣椒，花椒往往整道省略，花生加多，淋一层更亮的糖醋汁。来美的川人有时把美式版称为"不麻的宫保"——少了花椒的麻，整道菜就变了。话说回来：做得好的美式宫保鸡丁是好菜。我们不当警察。

Fortune cookie (签语饼)签语饼

Not Chinese. See the standalone essay. A Japanese rice-cracker that became the souvenir of American Chinese food via a 1900s San Francisco accident of demographics. It is a real food. It is not a real Chinese food.不是中国的。详见单篇。一种日本米饼，因为 20 世纪初旧金山的一桩人口学意外，变成了美式中餐的"纪念品"。它是真食物。但不是真中餐。

A frame for thinking about it想这件事的一个角度

Diaspora kitchens are a normal kind of cuisine. Italian-American food is its own kitchen — chicken parmigiana does not exist in Italy and that does not make it not Italian-American. British-Indian curry is its own kitchen — tikka masala was invented in Glasgow and that does not make it not British-Indian. American Chinese is exactly the same kind of phenomenon. It is born of specific people in specific cities navigating specific pressures: 1880s exclusion-era West Coast, 1930s Lower Manhattan, 1970s suburban strip-mall America. To honor it is to take its history seriously.流散厨房是一种正常的菜系。意美餐是它自己——意大利没有 chicken parmigiana，但这并不让它不是意美餐。英印餐是它自己——tikka masala 在格拉斯哥发明，但这并不让它不是英印餐。美式中餐是完全一样的现象。它是由具体的人、在具体的城市、面对具体的压力做出来的：1880 年代排华法案下的西海岸，1930 年代曼哈顿下城，1970 年代美国郊区购物街。要尊重它，就要认真对待它的历史。
