A capital, in snacks · Bei一座京城 · 北

Beijing · 北京

Beijing snacks like an empire. A small plate at the bus stop. Lamb skewers down a hutong. Fermented mung-bean juice that loyalists drink and tourists regret. The city eats in fragments.北京吃零嘴像吃帝国。公交车站旁一小份。胡同里几串烤羊肉。豆汁儿——老北京人喝，游客后悔——这座城以"零碎"吃饭。

Beijing is one of the few Chinese cities where the term "street food" still aligns more or less with what it once meant. The capital was, until the mid-1990s, a city of small dedicated shops and hawkers selling one thing each: a roujiamo cart, a bean-jelly stall, a sesame-cake oven, a man who only sold candied haw. Many of those shops are gone. Many survived as walk-up windows in slightly less photogenic concrete. A patient walk through Niujie, Hufangqiao, or Houhai will still turn up most of them.北京是中国少数几座"街食"这个词还沾边的城市。直到 1990 年代中期，京城还以单品小店、小贩为主——一辆肉夹馍车、一档凉粉摊、一只芝麻烧饼炉、一个只卖糖葫芦的人。许多店没了。许多以"窗口"形式残存在水泥楼里。耐心走过牛街、虎坊桥、后海，大半还能找回来。

6:30 a.m. — the jian bing cart06:30 — 煎饼摊

Beijing's defining breakfast is the jian bing (煎饼) — a thin millet-and-wheat crepe spread on a round cast-iron griddle, smeared with a fermented bean sauce and a sweet hoisin, sprinkled with scallions and cilantro, cracked-egged on top, layered with a crisp wonton sheet (薄脆), folded, cut once, handed to you in three minutes for eight yuan. The technique is in the wrist: the batter must be spread thin enough that the crepe is translucent. A jian bing on the way to the metro at 6:45 a.m. is Beijing's most universal experience.北京的招牌早餐是煎饼——小米加面粉的薄糊摊在铁鏊上，刷上甜面酱与海鲜酱，撒葱花香菜，磕一只蛋，铺一片薄脆，对折切开，三分钟出餐，八块钱递到手上。技术在手腕：糊要刷得薄到饼成半透。早上六点四十五赶地铁路上一只煎饼，是这座城最普遍的体验。

11:00 a.m. — the bean juice and pickle11:00 — 豆汁儿与焦圈

Doujir (豆汁儿) — fermented mung-bean juice — is Beijing's most divisive food. A milky-grey, mildly sour liquid produced as a by-product of mung-bean starch noodle making, served warm in a glazed ceramic bowl with a side of jiao quan (焦圈, a fried bread ring) and a small dish of pickled vegetables. Beijingers either drink it daily or refuse to discuss it. Outsiders generally hate it on the first sip; some come around. We did, slowly, over five visits.豆汁儿——发酵的绿豆浆水——是北京最具争议的食物。乳白偏灰、略酸的液体，是绿豆做粉条剩下的副产品，热饮，配焦圈与一碟咸菜。北京人要么天天喝，要么不愿讨论。外地人头一口大都受不了；少数人能"喝得回来"。我们去了五次才慢慢能接受。

7:30 p.m. — the lamb skewer alley19:30 — 羊肉串巷子

Beijing's lamb-skewer style descends from Xinjiang Uyghur cooking but has been Beijing-ified: smaller skewers, more uniform cubes of fat and meat, more cumin, less chili. Niujie — the historic Hui-Muslim quarter — is where the best alleys still serve them under hanging red lanterns, off charcoal-fired pits. A serious round is 30 skewers between two people, with a flatbread, a salted yogurt, and a beer. Add a plate of grilled flatcake (肉夹馕) and you have spent fifty yuan.北京的烤羊肉串源自新疆维吾尔，但被本地化了：签子更小、肥瘦更匀、孜然更重、辣减半。牛街——回民老坊——的几条小巷子，红灯笼下、炭炉前，仍是城里最对的烤肉点。两人吃就点 30 串、一只馕、一碗咸奶、一瓶啤酒。再点一份肉馕，你刚好花掉五十块。

9:00 p.m. — old-Beijing snacks (Lu Bin)21:00 — 老北京小吃（簋街）

A street like Gui Jie (簋街) or Hou Hai is, by 9 p.m., a parade of "old Beijing snacks" (老北京小吃): copper-pot mutton hot pot (涮羊肉), boiled tripe (爆肚), fermented mung-bean stewed pork (卤煮火烧), pea-flour cake (豌豆黄), candied haw on a stick (糖葫芦). The terminology is its own world: "explode-tripe" (爆肚) is sheep stomach blanched for exactly 12 seconds, so it crunches without going chewy. Halve the timing and it's underdone; double it and it's rubber. Watching a Niujie boss flip the basket out at the exact 12 is a small piece of expertise the city should be more proud of.簋街、后海这样的街，到九点会变成"老北京小吃"巡礼：铜锅涮羊肉、爆肚、卤煮火烧、豌豆黄、糖葫芦。术语自成体系——"爆肚"是把羊肚下沸水恰好烫 12 秒，要脆不要老。再短则生，再长则胶。看牛街老板掐着 12 秒抖筐起锅，是一门这座城应该更自豪一点的手艺。
