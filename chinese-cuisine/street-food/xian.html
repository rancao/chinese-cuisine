Xi'an · 西安

The Muslim Quarter at midnight. Persimmon cakes fried in goose fat. Cold-skin noodles that taste of vinegar and the desert. A city that's been feeding travelers for thirteen hundred years.回坊的午夜。鹅油煎柿子饼。一口下去全是醋与沙漠味的凉皮。这座城给路上的人喂饭，喂了一千三百年。

Xi'an was the eastern terminus of the Silk Road and capital of the Tang dynasty when the Tang was the largest city in the world. The Hui Muslim Quarter (回民街) sits just north of the Drum Tower and is the spiritual heart of the city's street food: ten centuries of trade with Persia and Central Asia compressed into a few crowded blocks. The food here is wheat-belt, lamb-heavy, vinegar-bright, and almost never quiet.西安是丝绸之路的东端，唐朝时为世界第一大城。鼓楼以北的回民街，是这座城街食的精神中心：一千年与波斯、中亚通商的历史，被压进几条挤满人的小街。这里的食物是麦区的、羊为主的、醋点亮的，几乎从不安静。

7:00 p.m. — roujiamo, the original19:00 — 肉夹馍，本尊

A Xi'an roujiamo (肉夹馍) is not the franchise sandwich you may have eaten in Shanghai or New York. The flatbread, called bai ji mo (白吉馍), is hand-formed, half-fermented, fired in a charcoal-heated clay oven so the outside crackles and the inside stays steamy. The filling is pork shoulder slow-braised for four hours with twenty-odd spices in a master stock that, like the Chaoshan brine, is never emptied. The boss chops the meat with a heavy cleaver right into the split bread, drips a spoonful of the braising liquid in, and hands it to you in a small paper sleeve. The whole transaction takes twenty seconds and costs eleven yuan.西安的肉夹馍不是你在上海或纽约吃过的连锁三明治。饼叫白吉馍，手压、半发酵、在炭烧泥炉里烤到外脆、内还冒蒸汽。馅是猪肩肉与二十多味香料在一锅"老汤"里慢炖四小时——这锅汤跟潮汕卤水一样，永不见底。老板用厚刀直接在劈开的馍上剁肉、滴一勺卤汁，套个小纸袋递给你。整套二十秒，十一块。

8:30 p.m. — liang pi, the noodles you can drink20:30 — 凉皮，一种能喝下去的面

A bowl of Xi'an liang pi (凉皮) is the most refreshing thing in the city's pantry. Wheat starch is washed from a dough into a watery slurry, steamed in shallow trays into translucent sheets, then cut into wide noodles. They are dressed cold with sesame paste, garlic water, dark Shanxi vinegar, sugar, soy, a drizzle of chili oil, and a handful of cilantro and bean sprouts. The whole bowl tastes simultaneously of summer and the desert wind. Eat with a single hand-fired wheat patty (麻酱凉皮配饼).西安的凉皮是这座城里最清爽的一碗。面粉揉成团再泡水分离出淀粉浆，蒸成半透明的片，切宽条。冷拌：芝麻酱、蒜水、山西陈醋、糖、酱油、一勺辣油、香菜与豆芽。整碗喝下去同时是夏天与沙漠的风。配一只手烤面饼（麻酱凉皮配饼）。

10:00 p.m. — yang rou pao mo22:00 — 羊肉泡馍

Yang rou pao mo (羊肉泡馍) is one of Xi'an's three or four oldest dishes and possibly its most ritualized. You receive an empty bowl and two dense flatbreads. You tear the flatbreads into pieces the size of soybeans with your fingers — this is the test; the smaller and more even, the more practiced the diner — and hand the bowl back. The kitchen ladles a deeply concentrated mutton broth, vermicelli, and tender mutton on top of your hand-torn bread. The bread soaks but does not dissolve. The result is somewhere between a stew, a soup, and a dumpling filling. With a side of pickled garlic and chili paste.羊肉泡馍是西安最古老的三四道菜之一，也大概是最仪式化的。客人拿到空碗与两只死面馍。要用手把馍掰成黄豆大小——这是考验，越小越均匀越说明你常吃——再把碗递回厨房。厨房浇上炖了几小时的羊汤、粉丝、嫩羊肉。馍吸了汤却不会化掉。结果介于炖菜、汤、与饺子馅之间。配糖蒜与辣酱。

11:30 p.m. — the goose-fat persimmon cake23:30 — 鹅油柿子饼

A Xi'an autumn produces large, glossy, almost translucent persimmons. A small cart along Beiyuanmen mashes them — skin, flesh, seeds removed — into a dough with flour, fills the dough with osmanthus-sugar paste, and fries the cakes in goose fat. The result is a small golden pastry, sweet but not cloying, with the faintest perfume of goose. Sold by the piece, six yuan, hot. Open until 1 a.m.西安的秋天产那种亮、近半透的大柿子。北院门一带的小摊会把它去皮去籽碾成糊，与面粉揉成饼，包桂花糖馅，鹅油里现煎。一只小金黄饼，甜不腻，淡淡一缕鹅香。论只卖，六块一只，刚出锅。卖到凌晨一点。

1:00 a.m. — biangbiang noodles, written凌晨 1:00 — biang 字面

A late-night bowl of biangbiang mian (biangbiang 面) is the punctuation mark of any Xi'an meal. Belt-wide noodles, hand-slapped on the counter (the sound — "biang" — gave the dish its name), boiled briefly, topped with raw garlic, scallion, a generous spoon of dried chili flakes, and finally a ladle of smoking-hot oil poured over the top so the chili crackles. Add a tablespoon of Shanxi black vinegar. Stir. Eat with chopsticks the diameter of pencils. This is Xi'an in a bowl.深夜来一碗 biangbiang 面，是任何一顿西安餐的句号。腰带宽的面，在案板上摔出"biang"的声音——名字就是这么来的——下锅短煮，铺生蒜、葱、一大勺辣椒面，最后浇一勺烧到冒烟的热油，辣椒哗啦响。再淋一勺山西陈醋。拌开。用铅笔粗的筷子吃。这一碗，就是西安。
