A night-walk · Chuan一段夜路 · 川

Chengdu · 成都

Chengdu does not eat. Chengdu snacks. The city has a near-mystical conviction that one more dish will fit, served on one more plate, washed down with one more beer, until the sun comes up.成都不吃饭。成都"加餐"。这座城带点神秘色彩地相信——再来一盘装得下，再来一瓶啤酒压得住——一路加到天亮。

We walked Chengdu for three nights in late September. The plan was to map a single street's worth of stalls; the city did not cooperate. By 9 p.m. our notebook had thirty entries and we had not yet left the third block. What follows is a partial record of one stretch, from the Wuhou Temple metro exit to the Yulin neighborhood, beginning at 7 p.m. and ending around two in the morning.九月末，我们在成都连走了三个晚上。原本计划是把一条街的摊子摸清，城里不配合。九点钟，本子上已经记了三十样，我们还没走出第三个街区。下面是其中一段的不完整记录——从武侯祠地铁站出来一直走到玉林，从傍晚七点写到凌晨两点左右。

7:10 p.m. — the skewer stall19:10 — 串串摊

A chuan chuan (串串) stall sits at the corner of every Chengdu intersection that pretends to be busy. The format is simple: a refrigerated rack of bamboo-skewered ingredients (lotus root, quail egg, beef tripe, duck intestine, enoki mushroom, slabs of luncheon meat), priced 0.5 to 2 yuan per stick. You pick your own, hand the bundle to the boss, and watch them simmer briefly in a pot of beef-tallow ma la broth. They are returned to you in a steel basket, the skewers stripped onto your plate by the boss with practiced grace. Add dry chili powder; add the famous "house oil" if it's any good. A stack of empty skewers grows on the table. The bill is the height of the stack times the per-stick price.在成都任何一个稍微像样的路口，转角都有一摊串串。形式很简单：冷柜里插好竹签的食材（莲藕、鹌鹑蛋、毛肚、鸭肠、金针菇、午餐肉片），每串五毛到两块。客人自选，把一把递给老板，老板下牛油麻辣锅烫一下，再用钢盘端上来，签子甩到你盘里。撒干辣椒面、淋"家油"——如果这家会做的话。空签子在桌角堆起来。结账，按签子高度乘单价。

8:40 p.m. — the cold-dish counter20:40 — 冷淡杯

A leng dan bei (冷淡杯, "cold-cup beer-snack") shop is Chengdu's most underrated institution. It is a small storefront — sometimes three plastic stools deep — that sells around twenty pre-made cold dishes, each in a stainless steel tray under glass: ma la beef tripe, red-oil chicken, smashed cucumber, peanuts in five-spice, drunken pig ear, cold rabbit, jellied head cheese, century-egg tofu. You point. The boss spoons two yuan worth of each onto small saucers; you pile them on your tiny table; you drink Snow beer (雪花啤酒) from glass bottles you stack on the curb. The whole meal arrives in eight minutes and costs forty yuan."冷淡杯"——成都最被低估的小铺子——是一家小门面，常常只摆得下三只塑料矮凳，柜玻璃下放着二十来样事先做好的凉菜：麻辣牛百叶、红油鸡块、拍黄瓜、五香花生、卤猪耳、凉拌兔丁、皮冻、皮蛋豆腐。客人点指头，老板按"两块钱一样"舀进小碟。小碟堆满桌，雪花啤酒的瓶子在路边码起来。整顿八分钟出齐，四十块。

10:15 p.m. — the rabbit-head shop22:15 — 兔头铺

Chengdu eats more rabbit per capita than anywhere on earth — three rabbits per resident per year, by a 2018 industry estimate. The signature snack is the rabbit head, sold cold by the piece, in two styles: ma la (with a numbing chili rub) or wu xiang (with a five-spice braise). Eating one is its own technique: you crack it open, suck the cheek meat, retrieve the tongue, work the small jaw. A serious diner will eat three in a row, drink a beer, and order three more. We managed two and admitted defeat.成都人均吃兔量是全球第一——2018 年行业估算每人一年三只。代表小吃是凉拌兔头，按只卖，两种做法：麻辣味或五香卤。吃一只是一门技术：先掰开，吸两腮，掏舌头，再处理小颌。认真的食客一口气吃三只，灌一瓶啤酒，再点三只。我们撑到两只就投降了。

11:50 p.m. — the noodle shop that doesn't close23:50 — 不打烊的面馆

A serious Chengdu street has at least one 24-hour noodle shop. The order, by gravity, is dan dan (担担), tian shui mian (甜水面, sweet-water noodles), or fu qi fei pian (夫妻肺片, husband-and-wife offal slices). Tian shui mian is the surprise of the menu: thick chewy noodles dressed only in sweet soy, garlic-water, sesame paste, and a single deep red chili oil. Half the table eats it in silence; it is that good. Husband-and-wife slices — beef shank, beef tripe, beef tongue, beef heart, all in a chili-and-peppercorn dressing — is a 1950s Chengdu invention named after the couple who pushed the original cart.一条像样的成都街，至少有一家 24 小时面馆。点单的引力顺序：担担面、甜水面、夫妻肺片。甜水面是菜单上的"惊喜"——粗韧的面只拌甜豉油、蒜水、麻酱、一勺深红辣油。半桌人安静吃完。夫妻肺片——牛腱、牛肚、牛舌、牛心，全部凉拌入麻辣汁——是 1950 年代成都一对夫妻推车叫卖时发明的，名字是他们的招牌。

1:20 a.m. — supper, technically凌晨 1:20 — "宵夜"，技术上

By one in the morning Chengdu's nominal dinner crowd has rolled into its "supper" (宵夜) crowd, and the menus shift slightly. Skewers stay open. The dishes that appear now are heavier: bullfrog hot-pot, river snail (田螺) tossed in a chili-and-perilla sauce, fried river crab, and the famous mao xue wang (毛血旺) — a dark cauldron of duck blood, tripe, eel, luncheon meat, and bean sprouts boiled in pure ma la broth and crowned with a fistful of charred dried chili. We ate at a corner shop where the boss kept a small cassette player going. The cassette was a 1992 Cantopop compilation; the city is bilingual in unexpected ways.凌晨一点，成都名义上"吃晚饭"的人换成了"吃宵夜"的人，菜单略偏。串串继续。这时上桌的菜更重：泡椒牛蛙、紫苏炒田螺、河蟹油爆、以及一锅响当当的毛血旺——鸭血、毛肚、鳝段、午餐肉、豆芽在纯麻辣汤里翻滚，最后浇一把炭烤干辣椒。我们停在一家路口铺子，老板放着一台小磁带机，磁带是 1992 年的粤语金曲合集。这座城在意想不到的地方双语。
