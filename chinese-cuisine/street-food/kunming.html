Kunming · 昆明

A 1,900-metre plateau city with a rice noodle on every corner, a pickle stand on every street, and — for six glorious weeks each summer — the smell of wild mushrooms over the entire city.海拔 1900 米的高原城。每个街角一碗米线，每条街一档腌菜。每年夏天有那么六周，整座城弥漫着野生菌的香气。

Kunming is a city the rest of China visits in retirement. The altitude keeps temperatures moderate year-round, the sky is bright, and the food is unhurried in a way the eastern megacities have not been for fifty years. Its street eating is correspondingly relaxed: rice noodles dominate, pickled-vegetable stalls outnumber convenience stores, and during the brief mushroom season the entire downtown re-organizes around fungi.昆明是中国其他城市退休时来住的地方。海拔让全年气温温和，天蓝得不像话，吃饭的节奏比东部任何一座大城都慢了半拍——慢了五十年。街上吃饭也跟着松：米线占主导，腌菜摊比便利店还多；菌季那几周，整个市中心围着菌子重新组织。

8:00 a.m. — xiao guo mi xian08:00 — 小锅米线

Most Kunming breakfasts are a bowl of xiao guo mi xian (小锅米线) — a single-portion noodle stew cooked to order in a small copper pot. Rice noodles, a ladle of pork stock, a pinch of mince, a spoonful of doubanjiang, pickled mustard greens, leek, a generous sprinkle of chili. The pot goes over a high flame for ninety seconds; the bowl arrives at the table still bubbling. The whole transaction costs ten yuan and takes three minutes. Eat in a small shop with three benches, before work.昆明早餐多半是一碗小锅米线——单人份的米线汤，现下小铜锅煮。米线、一勺猪汤、一撮肉末、一勺豆瓣酱、酸腌菜、韭菜、辣椒撒大方。火上九十秒，端到桌还在咕嘟。整套十块钱，三分钟搞定。在一家有三条长凳的小铺子吃，上班之前。

11:30 a.m. — the pickle stall11:30 — 腌菜摊

A Kunming pickle stall — a wooden cart with twenty open jars — is its own ecosystem. Pickled radish, pickled cabbage, pickled chili (the famous yuan jiao, 圆椒), pickled mustard greens at three stages of fermentation, pickled garlic, pickled lotus root, pickled bamboo shoot, salted preserved papaya. You point; the seller scoops; you walk on, eating from a paper cone. The function is not so much a meal as a structural element of every other meal: a Kunming household has six to ten different pickles on the table at any given dinner.昆明的腌菜摊——一辆木车上排着二十口敞口缸——是个小生态。腌萝卜、酸白菜、酸辣圆椒（出名那种）、不同发酵期的酸菜、糖蒜、腌藕、酸笋、盐渍木瓜。指一指，老板舀一勺，你纸袋装好边走边吃。它不当一顿饭，是其他每一顿饭的结构件——昆明人家晚饭桌上常摆六到十样腌菜。

June–July only — the mushroom season仅六七月 — 菌季

For roughly six weeks every summer, Kunming undergoes a city-wide reorientation around wild fungi. The Tuanjie (团结) and Mushui (木水) wholesale markets open dedicated mushroom halls; restaurants reprint menus weekly; office workers leave at 5 p.m. specifically to get to a market while the day's haul is still good. The cult species — porcini, matsutake, termite mushroom, morels, chicken-of-the-mountain — each get their own table at the wholesale market, their own price-per-kilo display, and their own warning. A serious Kunming dinner during this window may feature four or five species in one meal, each cooked differently — soup, stir-fry, stew, clear broth. The dishes go untouched for ten minutes after they arrive, because some species, undercooked, produce visions. The host will not allow you to start. This is correct.每年夏天大约六周，昆明全城为野生菌重新排版。团结、木水批发市场开出专门菌厅；餐厅每周改菜单；上班的人五点准时下班——只为赶在当天的菌还新鲜时到市场。明星种——牛肝菌、松茸、鸡枞、羊肚菌、青头菌——每一种在批发市场都有自己专柜、独立每公斤价、以及一块"注意事项"小牌。菌季的一桌饭可能同时上四五种菌，做法不同——煲汤、爆炒、炖煮、清汤。上桌后十分钟内主人不许动筷，因为某些种没炖透会让人"看见小人"。这是对的。

7:00 p.m. — the grill street, Wenlin19:00 — 文林街烧烤

Kunming's serious evening grilling lives on Wenlin Jie (文林街), just outside Yunnan University. Charcoal-grilled tofu cubes (烧豆腐) are the local specialty: small white squares of fermented tofu blistered over flame until the inside is pillowy and the outside skin contracts and tightens. You pluck them off the iron with chopsticks and dip into a wet sauce of soy, chili oil, fermented chili, and bone-marrow oil. A dedicated regular eats fifty in an evening and the boss counts them by the toothpicks on your plate. Add grilled potato slices, grilled mint leaves, grilled mountain pepper, grilled bee larvae (occasionally), and a bottle of Dali Erguotou-tier baijiu.昆明真正的夜烧烤在文林街，云大旁边。本地特色是烧豆腐——小块臭豆腐在炭火上烤到外皮收紧、内心鼓胀。客人用筷子从铁架上一只只夹下来，蘸调好的湿料：酱油、辣油、糟辣椒、骨髓油。常客一晚吃五十只是常事，老板按你盘子里的牙签数收钱。再加烤洋芋、烤薄荷叶、烤山椒、偶尔来一份蜂蛹烤；外加一瓶大理产的二锅头级白酒。
