Lane food · Hu弄堂 · 沪

Shanghai · 上海

Pan-fried buns at six in the morning. Scallion-oil noodles at one in the afternoon. Drunken crab in October. A city that does its real eating not on streets but in lanes.清晨六点的生煎。下午一点的葱油拌面。十月的醉蟹。这座城真正的吃，发生的不是在街上，而是在弄堂里。

Shanghai is, in 2026, two cities. The first is the global metropolis of Pudong skyscrapers, ten-yuan espresso, and tasting-menu restaurants quoting French and Japanese kitchens. The second is the lane city — the longtang (弄堂) neighborhoods of Jing'an, Xuhui, and Huangpu, where two-story shikumen tenements wrap interior alleys, and a quiet, persistent food culture has gone on more or less unchanged since the 1920s. We are interested in the second.2026 年的上海是两座城。第一座是浦东的摩天楼、十块钱的浓缩、还有把法式日式高级餐厅做了又做的城市。第二座是弄堂里的城——静安、徐汇、黄浦那些石库门里弄，二层小楼围出内里通道，从 1920 年代以来一直安静地吃自己的那一套。我们感兴趣的是后者。

6:30 a.m. — sheng jian bao06:30 — 生煎包

A Shanghai morning is incomplete without sheng jian bao (生煎包) — pork dumplings the size of a child's fist, half-leavened wheat dough on top, raw bottom seared in oil and a splash of water until crisp. The bun's interior holds a small reservoir of aspic — meat jelly added cold to the filling — that melts into broth as the bun cooks. Eating one is a technique: bite the seam, sip the broth, eat the rest. Try to do it in the wrong order and the broth runs down your chin. Locals manage four. We managed three.没有生煎包的上海早晨，不算早晨。生煎是孩子拳头大的猪肉包，发面在上、生底煎黄、最后一勺水烧出底脆。馅里事先冻了一小块肉皮冻，煎熟时化成汤汁，藏在包子里。吃法是技术：先咬开褶口，吸汤，再吃包子。顺序错了，汤就顺着下巴流。本地人一气吃四只，我们撑到三只。

7:30 a.m. — the four kings of breakfast07:30 — 四大金刚

Old Shanghai calls the four canonical breakfast items "the four kings" (四大金刚): da bing (a flatbread, sesame on the outside, often with scallion or sweetened with sugar), you tiao (deep-fried bread sticks), zi fan (a sticky-rice roll wrapped around a you tiao, optional pork floss), and dou jiang (warm soy milk, sweet or salty). The salty version of dou jiang — with a splash of vinegar, chili oil, dried shrimp, pickled vegetable, and crumbled you tiao — is a curdy, savory thing that startles most non-Shanghainese and converts about half of them. A complete set of four costs around 14 yuan.老上海把早餐四大件叫"四大金刚"：大饼（外贴芝麻、有咸有甜）、油条、粢饭（糯米团裹油条，可加肉松）、豆浆（甜咸两路）。咸豆浆——撒醋、辣油、虾皮、榨菜、再掰碎油条——是凝絮絮的咸鲜口，能吓退一半外地人，又能转化另一半。一套四大金刚约十四块。

1:00 p.m. — scallion oil noodles13:00 — 葱油拌面

A lane lunch in Shanghai is often cong you ban mian (葱油拌面) — scallion-oil noodles. The technique is elemental: scallions are slow-fried in neutral oil until they turn deep brown and crisp, the oil takes on their flavor, both are reserved. Thin wheat noodles are boiled, drained, dressed with the scallion oil, the fried scallions, a spoonful of light soy, a spoonful of dark soy, a pinch of sugar, and nothing else. Toss. Eat. The dish is what Shanghai's kitchen does best: technical restraint, four ingredients, one bowl that tastes like more.弄堂中午常见的是葱油拌面。手法极朴：青葱在中性油里慢炒到深褐酥脆，葱与葱油都留着。细面下锅煮、捞出、淋葱油、撒葱酥、一勺生抽一勺老抽、一捏糖，别的不放。拌开。吃。这道菜代表了上海厨房最好的一面——克制，四样东西，一只碗吃出比看着多的味道。

3:00 p.m. — the wonton, in Shanghai dialect15:00 — 上海方言里的馄饨

A Shanghai wonton (小馄饨) is small enough that fifteen of them fit comfortably in a bowl. The skin is so thin it floats translucent in the broth. The filling is a marble of pork. The broth is light — chicken stock, lard, a curl of dried shrimp, scallion, white pepper, and a thread of nori. Eaten as a between-meal pick-me-up at any hour. The larger Shanghai wonton (大馄饨) — with shepherd's purse and pork — is closer to a dumpling and earns its own bowl, not its own broth.上海小馄饨小到一碗里能放下十五只。皮薄到漂在汤里能透光，馅是猪肉小弹珠。汤是清的——鸡汤、猪油、一缕虾皮、葱花、白胡椒、一丝紫菜。任何时段当点心吃。大馄饨——荠菜猪肉馅——更接近饺子，自己一碗，不计汤。

October only — drunken crab仅十月 — 醉蟹

From mid-September to early November, the Yangcheng Lake hairy crab (大闸蟹) is the dominant subject of Shanghai conversation. The traditional treatment is to steam them with shiso (perilla) and serve with vinegar-ginger. The lane-food treatment is more local: drunken crab (醉蟹), a smaller river crab marinated in shaoxing rice wine, soy, scallion, and Sichuan peppercorn for at least 48 hours. Eaten cold, raw, slightly tipsy. Not for everyone — Westerners often blink — but irreplaceable to a generation of Shanghainese aunts.九月中到十一月初，阳澄湖大闸蟹是上海话题的主角。正宗做法是紫苏蒸，配姜醋。弄堂里更地道的一种是醉蟹——更小的河蟹用绍兴酒、酱油、葱、花椒泡至少 48 小时。冷的、生的、微醺的。不是所有人都吃得下——外国人常常皱眉——但对上海一代老阿姨来说，无可替代。
