A continent on a plate. 一盘饭里的大陆

From the smoke of a Chengdu hotpot to the hush of a Hakka stew, the kitchens of China are eight worlds, not one. This is an atlas of those worlds — region by region, season by season, ingredient by ingredient. 从成都火锅的辛香雾气，到客家老火汤的静默炖煮，中国的厨房不是一种味道，而是八个截然不同的世界。这本田野志按地域、节令与食材，将它们一一摊开。

Five ways into a vast kitchen.五种走进中国厨房的方式

There is no single Chinese cuisine. We chose five lenses — geography, the street, the pantry, the diaspora, and the calendar — through which a continent's food becomes legible. 从来没有什么"中国菜"。我们用五个镜头——地域、街道、食材、流散与节令——让一片大陆的饮食变得可读。

A pot that never stops simmering.永不熄火的那一锅

What we are doing here我们在干什么

A field guide, not a recipe book.这是田野志，不是菜谱

The bookshelves are already full of Chinese recipes. They are not full of patient writing about why kelp goes into a Cantonese stock, or how Hakka women in Meizhou seasoned a clay pot for three generations, or what a Lanzhou noodle puller listens for in the dough.英文世界从不缺中国食谱。它缺的，是耐心地写——粤式高汤里为什么有海带，梅州客家女人怎么把砂锅养了三代，兰州拉面师傅捏着面团到底在听什么。

We took five angles — region, street, ingredient, diaspora, season — and built thirty-odd essays on top of them. The site is bilingual. It is meant to be read slowly, in any order.我们选了五个角度——地域、街道、食材、流散、节令——并在它们之上写了三十多篇文章。本站双语，慢慢读，随便从哪儿开始都行。
